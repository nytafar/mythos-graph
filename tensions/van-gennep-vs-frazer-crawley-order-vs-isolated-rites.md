# Van Gennep vs Frazer/Crawley — order-as-meaning vs isolated rites

## The disagreement

Van Gennep's central methodological claim is that the *order* of
rites is the meaning unit. The schema of preliminal-liminal-postliminal
is not a classification of individual rites; it is a discovery about
what happens to meaning when rites are seen in their sequence
([[van-gennep-rites-of-passage#^p027]], [[van-gennep-rites-of-passage#^p184]],
[[van-gennep-rites-of-passage#^p375]]).

Frazer (in *The Golden Bough*) and Crawley work the opposite move:
extract the rite from its ceremonial whole, classify by external
similarity, and produce an encyclopaedic taxonomy of comparable
practices. Van Gennep's diagnosis is direct: "their authors as a
rule include only those elements of a ceremony which serve their
purposes. Moreover, their classifications are usually based on
external similarities rather than on the dynamics of the rite"
([[van-gennep-rites-of-passage#^p024]]; see also
[[van-gennep-rites-of-passage#^p373]]–[[van-gennep-rites-of-passage#^p374]]).

The disagreement is methodological, not topical. They share data;
they differ on what makes a unit of analysis operative.

## Why operative

The canon-shape this tension challenges is *every* canon written
by extracting a single beat (the threshold, the ordeal, the
return) without holding the sequence together. A controlling-idea
written by extracting one customer testimonial out of the
customer-arc context is the same methodological mistake at brand
scale. A villain written without the customer-arc beat the villain
is meant to obstruct is the same mistake. A boon written without
the ordeal that earned it is the same mistake.

The corpus inherits van Gennep's discipline as a structural rule:
canon-units are sequences, not items. The workshop satellites that
work a single dimension still pressure-test for sequence-coherence
upstream and downstream. This tension is the lineage-anchor for
the [[rites-in-isolation|Rites in isolation]] anti-pattern.

## Pressure-test signature

The operative pressure-test for the order-as-meaning failure mode
lives at [[rites-in-isolation|Rites in isolation]] anti-pattern —
signature, what-to-look-for, diagnosis-voice are written for direct
satellite consumption there. This tension carries the methodological-
genealogy load (vG's both-builds-on-and-opposes relation to Frazer/
Crawley); the anti-pattern carries the operative-diagnostic load.
Workshop satellites fire on the anti-pattern; the tension is read
when the lineage-genealogy itself becomes the question.

## Resolution direction

Van Gennep wins methodologically. The corpus's structural rule
inherits this: canon is sequenced, not catalogued. Workshop
satellites work single dimensions but pressure-test against
upstream and downstream sequence-coherence by default.

The reason van Gennep also lists Frazer in his
[[van-gennep#builds_on]] is that he inherited Frazer's
data-collection discipline while opposing Frazer's analytic
extraction. Both sides hold; the workshop discipline absorbs
Frazer's empirical thoroughness inside van Gennep's order-of-rites
constraint.

## Contributions

### From [[van-gennep-rites-of-passage|The Rites of Passage]]

Stated most directly at [[van-gennep-rites-of-passage#^p184]]: "The
primary purpose of this book is precisely to react against the
'folkloristic' or 'anthropological' procedure, which consists of
extracting various rites — whether positive or negative — from a
set of ceremonies and considering them in isolation, thus removing
them from a context which gives them meaning and reveals their
position in a dynamic whole." Reinforced in the conclusions
([[van-gennep-rites-of-passage#^p373]]–[[van-gennep-rites-of-passage#^p375]])
where he names the lineage of fragment-extraction: Hartland,
Frazer, Crawley, Diels, Dieterich, Hertz — all noticed
similarities; none saw the ordering as the meaning unit.
