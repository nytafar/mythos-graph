# Eliade vs Norse — imitatio dei vs ancestor-imitation through ætt

## The disagreement

Eliade's paradigmatic-model runs *imitatio dei*. Myth is up with
ontology; it speaks only of realities, of what really happened, of
what was fully manifested, and these realities are sacred realities
([[eliade-sacred-and-profane#^p131]]). What gods did is what *is*;
what men do without divine model is "vain and illusory"
([[eliade-sacred-and-profane#^p131]]–[[eliade-sacred-and-profane#^p134]]).
The anchor sentence: "Religious man is not given; he makes himself,
by approaching the divine models" ([[eliade-sacred-and-profane#^p141]]).
The direction is *vertical*: the self is constituted by approach to
the divine paradigm. Self-becoming runs upward, from ordinary man
toward the imitated god.

Norse substrate runs ancestor-imitation through ætt. The self is
made by carrying the lineage forward — by living up to the forebears,
extending the ætt-line, holding what the fathers held, adding what
the next generation will inherit. The paradigm is not the imitated
god; it is the best-self-of-the-forebear. The direction is
*horizontal* (back through ætt, forward through descendants), not
vertical. The substrate's word for this is dense — *ættens æra*,
*holde gården*, *minne sin slekt* — and the operation is not
cosmological-religious but lineage-ethical: you are made by what
you owe upstream and what you transmit downstream.

The two paradigms are not equivalent under translation. *Imitatio
dei* asks "what god is the model?" Ancestor-imitation asks "what
ætt am I carrying, and what is my forebears' best self?" Eliade's
universalist register would absorb ancestor-imitation as a
specific case of paradigmatic-model — the divine ancestor as
paradigm. But the substrate's ancestor is not divinized; the
forebear is human, fallible, and the paradigm is *their best
realisation*, not their apotheosis.

The Norse counterparty is not yet source-read; named at the
minimum abstraction the tension needs.

## Why operative

For substrate-aware Norwegian canon working customer-arc and guide
dimensions, the direction of the paradigm is structural. An
imitatio-dei frame produces canon where the customer's "best self"
is patterned on a divine, heroic, or transcendent model — the
brand assists the customer in approach-to-paradigm, where paradigm
is upward and external. The boon language inherits this direction:
the customer becomes-divine, becomes-heroic, becomes-other-and-
greater.

An ancestor-imitation frame produces canon where the customer's
"best self" is *their forebear's best self* — the paradigm is the
lineage-realised, the gård kept, the ætt extended. The brand
assists the customer in approach-to-forebear, where the paradigm
is horizontal (your own line) and *human-fallible* (your forebear
was a person who made it, and you can too). The boon language
inherits this direction: the customer becomes-themself-fully,
becomes-their-line-realised, becomes-the-link-the-next-generation-
inherits.

A Norwegian-substrate brand whose customer-arc canon imports
imitatio-dei produces guide voice that performs sacred uplift the
customer reads as foreign register. The substrate's heroism is
husmannsånd — the lineage-realised, not the divine-imitated — and
guide language that pushes the customer toward divine model rather
than forebear-best-self mismatches the substrate. Operative for
the customer-arc and guide dimensions specifically.

For non-substrate brands, imitatio-dei is operative cleanly; the
disagreement fires at substrate-aware canon.

## Pressure-test signature

When canon's customer-arc claims the customer becomes their "best
self" by imitating a divine, heroic, or transcendent template
(Eliade-default), check whether the substrate's actual paradigm is
"your-forebear-best-self" instead. The brand's guide voice and its
boon language inherit this direction-difference at every dimension.

Concrete check: who is the customer's paradigm in canon? If the
paradigm is upward-and-external (the divine, the heroic, the
mastered version of someone else), the canon is running imitatio-
dei. If the paradigm is horizontal-and-internal (the forebear, the
ætt-best, the lineage-realised), the canon is running ancestor-
imitation. For Norwegian-substrate brands, ancestor-imitation is
the substrate's native shape; canon running imitatio-dei has
imported the universalist frame without substrate correction.

The check fires at customer-arc (where the paradigm structures the
journey) and guide (where the brand's voice models approach-to-
paradigm). It also surfaces at boon (what the customer becomes is
shaped by which paradigm they were imitating).

## Resolution direction

Substrate-aware Norwegian brands take ancestor-imitation; substrate-
neutral or universal-claiming brands can take imitatio-dei cleanly.
Both valid; both are paradigmatic-model operations, but the
direction (vertical-and-divine vs. horizontal-and-lineage) is
operative.

For some brands the *combination* is operative: the forebear's
best self *was itself* the imitator of a higher paradigm, so
ancestor-imitation contains imitatio-dei recursively. The
substrate-aware operative move is to start from forebear, not from
god — even when god is in the picture, the substrate carries the
god through the forebear who carried it.

This tension will sharpen when [[norse-cosmology]] substrate-source
reads land — particularly substrate material on ætt, slekt,
*ættens æra*, the sagas read as ancestor-paradigm rather than as
heroic-divine narrative.

### From [[eliade-sacred-and-profane|The Sacred and the Profane]]

Eliade's paradigmatic-model is named at maximum sharpness in Ch II.
Myth-as-ontology at [[eliade-sacred-and-profane#^p131]]: "myth is
up with ontology; it speaks only of realities, of what really
happened, of what was fully manifested." What gods did is what
*is* at [[eliade-sacred-and-profane#^p131]]–[[eliade-sacred-and-profane#^p134]];
what men do without divine model is "vain and illusory." The
anchor sentence at [[eliade-sacred-and-profane#^p141]]: "Religious
man is not given; he makes himself, by approaching the divine
models." The direction-of-self-construction is unambiguous:
*toward* the divine paradigm. Eliade's universalist register reads
this as the human universal; the substrate-side counterparty
disputes the *toward-the-divine* direction without disputing that
self-construction runs through paradigm.

The Norse counterparty has not yet been source-read; the
ancestor-imitation position is named at the minimum abstraction
the tension needs, anchored to the substrate-side concept
[[ætt-individuation]] which the corpus already carries. To be
sharpened when Norse-cosmology and saga-corpus substrate-sources
are read.
