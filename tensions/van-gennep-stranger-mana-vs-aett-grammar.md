# Van Gennep stranger-mana vs ætt grammar

## The disagreement

Van Gennep frames the stranger as a doubled figure: weak because
outside the group, but sacred because outside — endowed with magico-
religious power that rites must neutralise or convert
([[van-gennep-rites-of-passage#^p055]],
[[van-gennep-rites-of-passage#^p056]]). The framework is mana-shaped:
the stranger carries a charge, and the rites mediate the charge.

Norwegian substrate's *fremmed/gjest* distinction does not run on
mana. It runs on ætt-grammar — the social-structural fact that the
stranger is not embedded in the network of obligations the ætt
defines. The fremmed is not magico-religiously charged; he is
*structurally placeless*, and therefore subject to the host's
hospitality at the host's discretion. *Fremmedfrykt* is not fear of
sacred power; it is wariness toward the unknown obligation-grammar
the stranger carries, and the substrate's *gjestebud* is the
mechanism for binding the stranger into a known obligation through
shared meal and reciprocal future-gift.

Both frames produce similar surface rites (offering food, the
threshold-crossing ritual, the protective host-figure). Underneath,
the operative ontology differs: vG's is mana-based, the substrate
is reciprocity-based.

## Why operative

Brand canon assigning meaning to "the stranger" — the new customer,
the inbound user, the prospect — inherits one of these frames. A
mana-frame brand treats the new customer as charged: dangerous to
existing customers, sacred in their potential, subject to ritual
mediation. A reciprocity-frame brand treats the new customer as
structurally placeless: their incorporation is the establishment of
an obligation grammar (what the brand owes them, what they owe the
brand, what existing customers owe them, what they owe existing
customers).

Most digital brands reflexively run the mana-frame because the
*excitement of the new customer* maps cleanly onto sacred-charge
language. The substrate's reciprocity-frame is structurally sober
and correspondingly unsexy — it produces onboarding designed
around obligation-establishment rather than around the magic of
arrival.

## Pressure-test signature

When canon assigns meaning to the new customer or the prospect:
check whether the meaning is mana-shaped (charged, sacred-by-
outsideness, requires mediation to defuse-and-incorporate) or
reciprocity-shaped (structurally placeless, requires obligation-
grammar to be established).

Both can produce operative canon. The substrate-aware brand
should know which it is choosing. The error is taking the mana-
shape by default because it is more available in the marketing-
side vocabulary, when the brand's actual onboarding mechanics are
reciprocity-shaped.

## Resolution direction

For Norwegian-substrate-aware brands the reciprocity-frame is
substrate-native and more honest. The mana-frame can be held
adjacently when the brand also operates in a sphere where mana-
shape carries (e.g., status-economy brands where new customers
*are* charged with social distinction).

Substrate-vs-universal — always operative per the northern star.
The substrate-frame is allowed to *augment* the universal frame:
the substrate surfaces obligation-grammar that vG's mana-frame
does not name, and that augmentation is operative information for
canon-makers.

## Contributions

### From [[van-gennep-rites-of-passage|The Rites of Passage]]

Van Gennep's full statement is at
[[van-gennep-rites-of-passage#^p055]]–[[van-gennep-rites-of-passage#^p056]]:
"such a person is weak, because he is outside a given group or
society, but he is also strong, since he is in the sacred realm
with respect to the group's members." Frazer and Crawley are cited
as having attributed the rites "to magico-religious terror in his
presence." Van Gennep accepts this frame and extends it through Ch
III's full reception-rites typology. The substrate counterparty —
ætt-individuation, fremmed/gjest grammar — has not yet been
source-read; the disagreement is named here against substrate-known
position from the wave-3 substrate work, to be sharpened when a
substrate-source carrying ætt-grammar is read.
