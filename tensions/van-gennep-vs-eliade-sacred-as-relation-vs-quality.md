# Van Gennep vs Eliade — sacred-as-relation vs sacred-as-quality

## The disagreement

Van Gennep treats sacredness as a *relation*. Sacredness is not an
attribute of an object, place, or person; it is brought into play by
the configuration of a particular situation. The same individual is
profane to her household and sacred to the strangers she meets at the
frontier. The "magic circles pivot" as a person moves from one
position in society to another ([[van-gennep-rites-of-passage#^p030]]).

Eliade treats hierophany as a manifestation of the sacred *as
quality*: the sacred reveals itself in specific objects, places, and
times that thereby become sacred *as such*. Hierophany is "the
manifestation of something of a wholly different order, a reality
that does not belong to our world" ([[eliade-sacred-and-profane#^p007]]).
The sacred place is not sacred-relative-to a participant; the sacred's
manifestation *ontologically founds* the place
([[eliade-sacred-and-profane#^p018]]–[[eliade-sacred-and-profane#^p020]]).
The Achilpa pole carries this at maximum sharpness: the pole is sacred
because the sacred manifested there, and when the pole breaks the
world ends ([[eliade-sacred-and-profane#^p042]]). Stone hierophany is
glossed as *ontophany* — the stone reveals "the irreducibility and
absoluteness of being" ([[eliade-sacred-and-profane#^p219]]). Mythic
disclosures are "primordial revelations" ([[eliade-sacred-and-profane#^p192]])
— the sacred discloses itself, and the disclosure has the structure of
revelation, not of pivot.

The distinction is not terminological. Van Gennep's sacred is a
field that pivots; Eliade's sacred is a property that descends. Both
sources stand fully anchored on opposite sides of the same axis.

## Why operative

Brand canon working in cosmology mode inherits one of two
metaphysics. If sacred is *relational*, then sacred space, sacred
time, and sacred objects in the brand's world get their charge from
the configuration the customer is in — not from the object's
inherent property. Onboarding moments become liminal because of the
customer's relation to the threshold, not because of any quality
the moment carries on its own. Marketing produces relational sacred
through configurations: the right customer, in the right state, at
the right boundary.

If sacred is a *quality*, the brand authors objects, places, and
times that *are* sacred — the homepage as temple, the ritual moment
as illud tempus, the founding artifact as hierophany. Marketing
produces quality-sacred by elevating the artifact, not the
configuration.

Most marketing-side cosmology defaults reflexively to quality-sacred
because objects are easier to author than configurations. Van
Gennep's discipline is the corrective. But Eliade's frame is the
corrective in the opposite direction: a brand that runs *only*
relational sacred underclaims the cosmology — the customer can pivot
into and out of the sacred, but nothing the brand does *is* sacred,
which leaves the brand-as-arena rather than brand-as-axis.

## Pressure-test signature

When canon claims that a moment, place, or artifact in the customer's
journey is sacred: check whether the claim is *relational* (sacred
because of the customer's position relative to it) or *qualitative*
(sacred as such). The sharper form: *is the brand's claim that this
moment IS sacred (Eliade), or that this moment is sacred IN RELATION
to the customer's position (vG)?* Both are valid; brittle canon
usually mismatches the claim and the delivery — claims quality-sacred
and delivers it through configuration, or claims relational-sacred
and authors it as if the artifact alone carries the charge.

The check has two halves: which one is the canon making, and does
the brand's onboarding/ritual structure deliver that one? A
quality-sacred claim with relational delivery will under-charge the
artifact; a relational-sacred claim authored as quality will
under-deliver because the configuration the participant must occupy
is missing.

## Resolution direction

Both stand. The choice is dimension-specific:

- mythos-rites and mythos-customer-arc lean van-Gennep — passage
  rituals are relational by their structure; the participant pivots,
  the rite enacts the pivot, the sacred is indexical to position
- mythos-cosmology can take either; substrate-aware brands often
  combine (gard-utgard is qualitatively sacred *and* the relation
  pivots when you cross it)
- mythos-sacred-calendar leans Eliade — illud tempus, the festival
  that recovers primordial mythical time
  ([[eliade-sacred-and-profane#^p085]], [[eliade-sacred-and-profane#^p101]]),
  is quality-sacred work
- mythos-villain and mythos-controlling-idea: dimension-neutral; the
  tension surfaces only when the canon move asserts that something
  *is* sacred or that a configuration *makes* sacred
- The wrong default is "all sacred is quality" — Eliade's frame
  applied without van Gennep's corrective produces brittle canon
  that imports cathedral-sacred into onboarding moments and
  underperforms; the symmetric wrong default is "all sacred is
  relation" — van Gennep without Eliade leaves the brand without an
  axis to stand on

vG's read flagged this from his side; this read anchors Eliade's
side. Both source-reads complete; the tension is now operative at
full weight, with no remaining sharpening event pending.

## Contributions

### From [[van-gennep-rites-of-passage|The Rites of Passage]]

The pivoting-of-sacred move is named at [[van-gennep-rites-of-passage#^p030]]:
"the 'magic circles' pivot, shifting as a person moves from one place
in society to another." Van Gennep makes the relational ontology
explicit and load-bearing — the schema of preliminal-liminal-postliminal
only works because sacredness can pivot under the rite.

### From [[eliade-sacred-and-profane|The Sacred and the Profane]]

Eliade carries the opposite side of the same axis at full weight.
Hierophany is named at [[eliade-sacred-and-profane#^p007]] as the
operative term for the entire corpus: "something sacred shows itself
to us." The quality-claim definition runs through the structural
move at [[eliade-sacred-and-profane#^p018]]–[[eliade-sacred-and-profane#^p020]]:
sacred space is *ontologically founded* by the manifestation, not
relationally constituted by a participant's position. Stone
hierophany at [[eliade-sacred-and-profane#^p219]] is glossed as
*ontophany* — pure quality-claim, the stone reveals the irreducibility
of being. Mythic disclosures at [[eliade-sacred-and-profane#^p192]]
are *primordial revelations*: the sacred discloses, the disclosure
has revelatory rather than relational structure.

The pivoting-of-sacred concept already carries the cross-source
treatment of this disagreement
([[pivoting-of-sacred]] — see "From [[eliade-sacred-and-profane]]"
section): Eliade allows that the sacred withdraws from places
(deus-otiosus pattern, [[eliade-sacred-and-profane#^p168]]–[[eliade-sacred-and-profane#^p173]])
and that the modern condition desacralizes the world
([[eliade-sacred-and-profane#^p243]]), but the sacred when present
is *what is real*, not *what is so-called by the participants*. Van
Gennep's pivot is real for Eliade only as the pivot in *human
awareness* of an ontological reality that itself does not pivot.
