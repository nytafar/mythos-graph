# Van Gennep vs Vogler — nested liminality vs flat 12-stage

## The disagreement

Van Gennep's schema is recursive. Any liminal phase can articulate
into its own preliminal-liminal-postliminal arc, and that
sub-articulation can recurse again. Betrothal is the liminal phase
between adolescence and marriage — and within betrothal, there are
its own three phases of separation, transition, and incorporation
([[van-gennep-rites-of-passage#^p028]]). The schema is fractal: it
applies at every scale a passage occurs.

Vogler's compression of Campbell into the twelve-stage *Writer's
Journey* takes the structure flat. Twelve stages in a single
sequence; each stage occurs once. Threshold-crossing happens at
Stage 5; ordeal happens at Stage 8; the journey runs linearly.
This compression is what makes the schema teachable to screenwriters
and applicable to a single-feature-length narrative arc.

The compression drops the recursion. Vogler's structure is not
wrong — it is van Gennep's schema with the nested case removed.

## Why operative

For brand canon working customer-arc at the *system* scale, the
nested-liminal property is load-bearing. A customer's full arc
through a brand may take years and contain multiple complete
passages — onboarding, first deep use, expansion, advocacy. Each
of those is its own complete preliminal-liminal-postliminal arc,
and the larger arc is the sequence of these complete sub-arcs.

A customer-arc canon that takes Vogler's flat twelve and applies
it to brand-scale produces canon that names threshold-crossing
once. The brand has only one threshold; the customer crosses it
during the trial signup; afterwards they are post-threshold. This
is the compression failure. A van-Gennep-disciplined customer-arc
notices that the post-trial-threshold customer is now in the
ordinary world of an *expanded* arc whose own threshold is the
upgrade decision, whose own ordeal is the integration into team
practice, and so on. The schema recurses because the customer's
life recurses.

## Pressure-test signature

When a canon write names a single threshold, ordeal, or
incorporation moment in customer-arc: check whether there are
nested instances at smaller and larger scales. The smaller-scale
check: what is the threshold-within-this-stage? The larger-scale
check: what is the threshold-of-which-this-stage-is-the-liminal-
period?

If the answer to either is "there isn't one — this is the only
threshold", the canon is treating the customer's life as a single
linear narrative. That is sometimes correct (early-stage product,
single-purchase product). For most brands operating across
expansion, retention, and advocacy, treating the journey as flat
is the Vogler-compression failure.

## Resolution direction

Van Gennep wins for system-scale customer-arc work. Vogler's flat
twelve is operative for narrative-scale (single feature, single
campaign, single onboarding sequence). They are not at war; they
work at different scales.

The error is *only* having Vogler's frame and applying it at
system-scale. The corrective is van Gennep's recursion property
held alongside Vogler's narrative discipline — Vogler at narrative
scale, van Gennep at system scale, and the system scale containing
multiple narrative scales as nested liminal phases.

This tension will sharpen when [[vogler|Christopher Vogler]] is source-read; the
flat-twelve construction will gain its own anchors and the
compression mechanism (what Vogler had to drop) will be specifiable.

## Contributions

### From [[van-gennep-rites-of-passage|The Rites of Passage]]

The recursion is stated at [[van-gennep-rites-of-passage#^p028]]:
"in certain ceremonial patterns where the transitional period is
sufficiently elaborated to constitute an independent state, the
arrangement is reduplicated. A betrothal forms a liminal period
between adolescence and marriage, but the passage from adolescence
to betrothal itself involves a special series of rites of separation,
a transition, and an incorporation into the betrothed condition." The
conclusions name this as one of three load-bearing theoretical
claims of the book ([[van-gennep-rites-of-passage#^p376]]) — that
"transitional periods which sometimes acquire a certain autonomy"
is what no prior scholarship had noticed and what makes the schema
fractal rather than linear.
