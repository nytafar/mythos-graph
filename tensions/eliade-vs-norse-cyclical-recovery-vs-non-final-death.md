# Eliade vs Norse — cyclical recovery vs non-final death

## The disagreement

Eliade's eternal return depends on cyclically-recoverable primordial
time. Sacred time is *reversible*: "a primordial mythical time made
present" through festival ([[eliade-sacred-and-profane#^p085]]).
Liturgical time is the reactualization of an event "in the
beginning" *in illo tempore* ([[eliade-sacred-and-profane#^p085]],
[[eliade-sacred-and-profane#^p101]]). The Babylonian *akitu* — New
Year as cosmogony repeated, Tiamat re-conquered, sins annihilated,
time reborn ([[eliade-sacred-and-profane#^p096]]–[[eliade-sacred-and-profane#^p100]])
— is the case at maximum sharpness. Sacred time is "succession of
eternities" ([[eliade-sacred-and-profane#^p119]]); the calendar's
mechanism is the periodic return to recoverable origin
([[eliade-sacred-and-profane#^p126]] — *nostalgia for paradise* as
religious nostalgia for the periodic return). The frame is
fundamentally *backward-looking* and *recovery-shaped*.

Norse cosmology has Ragnarök. The eschatological horizon is
irreversible: the wolves catch the sun and moon, Surt's fire consumes
the world, the gods fall, only Líf and Lífþrasir survive in a hidden
grove, Gimli is post-eschatological. The frame is fundamentally
*forward-looking* and *non-final-but-not-recoverable*: the cycle
does not return the lost world to itself — it ends, and what comes
after is *new*, not *recovered*. Death is non-final (Líf and
Lífþrasir, Baldr's return, the post-Ragnarök gods) but the world
that ended cannot be reactualized; it is gone.

The existing tension `van-gennep-symbolic-rebirth-vs-norse-non-final-death`
covers a different axis (vG's symbolic-rebirth vs. Norse death-as-
non-final at the rite scale). This tension covers the *cosmological*
axis: Eliade's recoverable primordial time vs. Norse irreversible
horizon at the calendar/cosmogony scale. The two cross-reference
without merging — one is rite-scale, this is cosmology-scale.

The Norse counterparty is not yet source-read in the corpus; named
at the minimum abstraction the tension needs.

## Why operative

For substrate-aware Norwegian canon working sacred-calendar mode,
the temporal frame the calendar runs on is structural. Eliade's
frame produces canon where each annual cycle returns the customer
to the recoverable origin: jul restores the world, jonsok recovers
midsummer's primordial sun, 17. mai reactualizes 1814. The calendar
"begins anew" each year; the brand's sacred-time work is recovery
of origin.

A Norse-substrate-aware frame produces canon where each annual
cycle is dense (jul, fastelavn, påske, 17. mai, jonsok, olsok,
høst-rytme, mørketid) AND eschatologically-anchored — the renewal
points forward toward an irreversible horizon, not back to a
recoverable origin. Jul holds against mørketid, knowing mørketid
returns and that one day the holding will fail; jonsok celebrates
the sun knowing the wolves are coming. The renewal is *real* but
*not recoverable* — what was lost is gone, and what is celebrated
is the holding-against-loss, not the recovery-of-origin.

A brand that imports Eliade's recovery-shaped sacred-calendar into
a substrate that runs forward-looking eschatology produces canon
that performs renewal without performing the substrate's actual
temporal work. The customer reads jul as Christmas-as-cosmogony
(Eliade's frame) where the substrate runs jul as light-against-
darkness-knowing-the-darkness-will-return (substrate frame).

## Pressure-test signature

When canon claims a sacred-calendar that "begins anew," "renews,"
or "reactualizes" each cycle: check whether the cyclical-renewal
frame fits the substrate. For Norse-substrate brands, the calendar
is dense AND eschatologically-anchored — the renewal points forward
toward an irreversible horizon, not back to a recoverable origin.

Concrete check: where does the canon name *what cannot be recovered*?
A canon that runs only recovery-shaped renewal has imported Eliade
without the substrate's eschatological correction. The substrate's
sacred-calendar work names both the renewal and the irreversible
loss the renewal holds against. Canon that names only the renewal
is performing Eliade-default; canon that names the renewal *and*
what-cannot-be-recovered is doing the substrate's actual work.

The signature fires at full operative weight when Norse-cosmology
substrate-sources are read; until then, partial anchor through the
substrate-side concept sections.

## Resolution direction

Substrate-aware Norwegian brands take the eschatologically-anchored
frame; substrate-neutral or universal-claiming brands can take
Eliade's recoverable-origin frame cleanly. The renewals are not
the same operation: Eliade's renewal *recovers* the lost; the
substrate's renewal *holds against* further loss while knowing the
holding will eventually fail.

For brand mythos-sacred-calendar work in Norwegian substrate, the
operative move is *both renewals named*: the sacred-calendar carries
recovery-shape (continuity with the broader Indo-European pattern
Eliade documents) AND eschatological-anchor (Ragnarök-shaped horizon
the substrate carries). Holding only one collapses the substrate's
texture.

This tension will sharpen when [[norse-cosmology]] substrate-source
reads land (Völuspá in particular, also Snorri's Gylfaginning section
on Ragnarök). Cross-reference: the rite-scale axis is
[[van-gennep-symbolic-rebirth-vs-norse-non-final-death]]; the
cosmology-scale axis is this node.

### From [[eliade-sacred-and-profane|The Sacred and the Profane]]

the corpus. Sacred time as reversible at
[[eliade-sacred-and-profane#^p085]]; *in illo tempore* at
[[eliade-sacred-and-profane#^p101]]; *akitu* and *Enuma elish* as
cosmogony-repeated at [[eliade-sacred-and-profane#^p096]]–[[eliade-sacred-and-profane#^p100]];

nostalgia for paradise as periodic-return at
[[eliade-sacred-and-profane#^p126]]. The chapter closes
([[eliade-sacred-and-profane#^p149]]) on the eternal-return as
religious man's *self-construction* — to live the divine model is to
re-enter the recoverable primordial time the model exists in. The
Judaeo-Christian rupture at [[eliade-sacred-and-profane#^p155]]–[[eliade-sacred-and-profane#^p156]]
is read as departure-from-recoverable-time; modern desacralized
duration ([[eliade-sacred-and-profane#^p157]]) is the failure mode
of cyclic-without-recovery.

The Norse counterparty has not yet been source-read; the
eschatological-horizon position is named at the minimum abstraction

Gimli as post-eschatological-not-restored-eden) which will sharpen
