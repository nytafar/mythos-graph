# Van Gennep magico-religious vs Norse contractual

## The disagreement

Van Gennep's working vocabulary fuses magic and religion into a
single category — *magico-religious* — because, in his frame, the
distinction between practice and theory is artificial: "the practice
and the theory are inseparable" and detaching them produces either
metaphysics (theory without practice) or science (practice without
appropriate theory) ([[van-gennep-rites-of-passage#^p031]]). The
framework reads social-binding rites (oath-swearing, gift-exchange,
vendetta-resolution) through this magico-religious lens.

Norwegian substrate's customary-law tradition runs many of the same
rites through a contractual frame: *handslag* binds because the
binding is socially-witnessed and legally-actionable, not because of
mana transfer. *Tingstedet* (the assembly site) is sacred *and*
juridical; the sacred is built from the legal layer's reliability
rather than the legal layer being a sacred extension. *Gjestebud*
is a contract for future reciprocal hospitality with codified terms,
not a magico-religious incorporation event. The substrate's frame is
not anti-religious — sacred remains operative — but the analytic
ground is contractual reciprocity, with sacred operating through
the contract rather than as the contract's substrate.

## Why operative

For substrate-aware brand canon working on incorporation, oath-like
moments, and binding rituals (the customer commitment moment, the
referral, the public pledge, the upgrade), the substrate's
contractual frame names what the magico-religious frame leaves
implicit: *what specific obligation has just been taken on, by
whom, with what enforcement mechanism*.

A magico-religious frame produces canon language about
incorporation moments that is mana-shaped and analytically vague.
A contractual frame produces canon language that names the
specific bidirectional obligations the moment establishes. Both
produce operative canon; they differ on which surface area the
canon must specify.

This tension is at lower intensity than the others — the
magico-religious frame is not actively wrong for substrate work,
but it under-specifies. The substrate is allowed to augment.

## Pressure-test signature

When canon names an incorporation moment, an oath-like commitment,
or a binding ritual: check whether the canon specifies the
*contractual layer* — what obligations are taken on, by whom, what
enforcement reciprocity exists. The magico-religious frame allows
the canon to feel charged without specifying the mechanics; the
contractual frame requires the mechanics.

The check is currently inactive — the corpus does not yet have
substrate-source-read material on Norse customary law (*Gulating*,
*Frostating*, Magnus Lagabøtes *landslov*) with anchors. Pressure-
test re-enables when those reads land.

## Resolution direction

Deferred. The augmentation is real; the corpus is not yet pressure-
test-ready. Lower priority than the other substrate-vs-universal
tensions because the magico-religious frame is operative even
without the substrate augmentation.

When a substrate-source on Norse customary law is read, this
tension graduates to operative. Until then: substrate-aware brand
canon should know that incorporation language can be specified
contractually, not only magico-religiously, and that the
contractual specification often produces sharper canon.

## Contributions

### From [[van-gennep-rites-of-passage|The Rites of Passage]]

Van Gennep settles the working vocabulary at
[[van-gennep-rites-of-passage#^p031]]: "Since the practice and the
theory are inseparable — the theory without the practice becoming
metaphysics, and the practice on the basis of a different theory
becoming science — the term I will at all times use is the
adjective *magico-religious*." The fusion is methodologically
deliberate, not accidental — it is the move that lets him treat
ritual sequence as the meaning unit without committing to either a
purely-religious or purely-magical theory of cause. The
substrate's contractual-reciprocity counterparty has not yet been
source-read; this tension is recorded as deferred for sharpening
once Norse customary-law substrate-sources are read.
