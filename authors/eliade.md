# Mircea Eliade

Romanian-American historian of religion (1907 Bucharest – 1986 Chicago). Trained in philosophy at Bucharest under Nae Ionescu and in Sanskrit at Calcutta under Surendranath Dasgupta; the Indian period (1928–1932) is structurally load-bearing for the lifework — the cross-cultural reach of *The Sacred and the Profane* (Mesopotamian, Indian, Polynesian, Kwakiutl, Australian, Christian) is methodologically continuous with the Indological apprenticeship. Took the chair of History of Religions at the University of Chicago in 1957, the same year *The Sacred and the Profane* appeared in English.

The corpus's most operative single source for the cosmology dimension and a load-bearing source for the sacred-calendar dimension. The vocabulary that runs through the engine — *hierophany* ([[eliade-sacred-and-profane#^p007]]), *axis mundi* ([[eliade-sacred-and-profane#^p045]]), *imago mundi* ([[eliade-sacred-and-profane#^p052]]), *illud tempus* ([[eliade-sacred-and-profane#^p101]]), *deus otiosus* ([[eliade-sacred-and-profane#^p168]]–[[eliade-sacred-and-profane#^p173]]), *body-house-cosmos* ([[eliade-sacred-and-profane#^p237]]) — is Eliade's. The lifework, the multivolume *History of Religious Ideas*, gives the same comparative-phenomenological method at full reach; *The Sacred and the Profane* is its short, exportable distillation.

Method: phenomenology of religion in the post-Otto current — the project is to recover the structures of religious experience as such, not to explain religion sociologically (Durkheim) or psychologically (Freud) or evolutionarily (Tylor, Frazer). Names Otto's *Das Heilige* (1917) explicitly as the direct ancestor at [[eliade-sacred-and-profane#^p001]]–[[eliade-sacred-and-profane#^p003]]; widens Otto's analysis from the irrational/numinous to *the sacred in its entirety* by opposition to the profane. Refuses Tylor and Frazer's nineteenth-century universalism of uniform mental reaction at [[eliade-sacred-and-profane#^p014]] explicitly: comparative juxtaposition is valid for *structure*, not for *content*; differences are conditioned by history, but the unity sought is structural. The Durkheimian sacred/profane dyad is the silent substrate — Eliade extends Durkheim's category from sociological fact to ontological mode without naming the inheritance, which is itself a methodological signature: the sacred becomes mode-of-being rather than collective representation.

For the engine: Eliade's universal-claiming reach is operative *and* must be pressure-tested against substrate sources. The claim that hierophany operates the same in Achilpa pole, Babylonian *bab apsu*, and Christian Eucharist is the move the cosmology dimension takes as ground; the substrate forks running the same passages against Norwegian-folkloric material (seter, gravhaug, Yggdrasil-as-gnawed-tree) will surface where Eliade's universal frame illuminates and where it flattens. Yggdrasil is named explicitly at [[eliade-sacred-and-profane#^p207]] — the substrate-pressure-test anchor.

Curator note: the universalist register is *the method*, not a contingent stylistic choice. Pressure-testing Eliade's universals from substrate ground is operative work, not corrective work. The substrate is parallel infrastructure (per northern star); Eliade gives the vocabulary and the structural anatomy of cosmological reach without which substrate's distinctive contributions cannot be named precisely. Resist the move that collapses substrate into Eliade's universal categories; equally, resist the move that treats Eliade's vocabulary as substitute for the substrate's own.
