# Émile Durkheim

[Skeleton — created during source-read of [[eliade-sacred-and-profane]] as
`builds_on` ancestor. Durkheim's *Les formes élémentaires de la vie religieuse*
(1912) installed the sacred/profane dyad as the operative analytical
distinction of religion — the substrate Eliade extends throughout
[[eliade-sacred-and-profane]] without ever naming the inheritance. Eliade
takes Durkheim's category and shifts its ontological status: where Durkheim
reads the sacred as collective representation, Eliade reads it as mode of
being ([[eliade-sacred-and-profane#^p012]]). The silence is itself the
methodological move — Eliade refines-by-substitution, replacing sociological
substrate with phenomenological ontology without polemic. A future Durkheim
source-read will likely produce a tension node on this axis (sacred-as-social-
fact vs sacred-as-mode-of-being). Body and full lineage populated when
Durkheim's own work is source-read.]
