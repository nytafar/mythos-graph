# Cosmic tree

## Operational definition

The world tree: tree as image of the cosmos, of inexhaustible life,
of regeneration through the rhythm of vegetation. Distinct from
[[axis-mundi]] (which the cosmic tree can serve as) and from a
mere botanical sacred tree (which carries hierophany without
encompassing the whole cosmos). The cosmic tree is the tree-image
that holds the entire cosmos: roots in the underworld, trunk on
earth, branches in heaven, fruit conferring immortality, guarded
by monsters, growing "in the distant land."

Operative consequence: the cosmic tree is the substrate-richest
single image of cosmic regeneration. Each substrate names its own
tree — Yggdrasil for the Norse, the gum-tree pole among the
Achilpa, the Hindu *aśvattha*, the Christian Tree of the Cross —
and each substrate's tree carries substrate-specific properties.
The brand whose cosmology is structured around a tree-image (or a
tree-analogue: a perennial root system, an inheritance, a
generative substrate) is using cosmic-tree language whether it
names the figure or not.

## Contributions

### From [[eliade-sacred-and-profane]]

Eliade introduces the cosmic tree in chapter III's section
"Symbolism of the Cosmic Tree and of Vegetation Cults"
[[eliade-sacred-and-profane#^p205]]–[[eliade-sacred-and-profane#^p209]],
extending forward from the terra-mater material.

The first operative claim at
[[eliade-sacred-and-profane#^p206]]: "the cosmos is a living
organism, which renews itself periodically. The mystery of the
inexhaustible appearance of life is bound up with the rhythmical
renewal of the cosmos. This is why the cosmos was imagined in the
form of a gigantic tree; the mode of being of the cosmos, and
first of all its capacity for endless regeneration, are
symbolically expressed by the life of the tree."

The tree-as-cipher claim immediately following: "As a natural
object, the tree could not suggest the whole of cosmic life; on
the level of profane experience its mode of being does not
coincide with the mode of being of the cosmos in all its
complexity. On the level of profane experience vegetable life
displays merely a series of births and deaths." Only the religious
vision sees more than birth-and-death in vegetation; the cosmic
tree is the religious tree-vision, not the botanical tree.

The Yggdrasil mention at
[[eliade-sacred-and-profane#^p207]]: "In addition to cosmic trees
like the Yggdrasil of Germanic mythology, the history of religions
records trees of life, of immortality, of knowledge, of youth, and
so on." This is the single passage in *The Sacred and the Profane*
where Eliade names Yggdrasil. He treats it as one instance of the
universal cosmic-tree pattern. The Norwegian substrate's
Yggdrasil-as-gnawed-by-Niðhǫggr property does not enter Eliade's
frame; for Eliade, Yggdrasil is the Germanic substrate's
expression of a universal claim about cosmic regeneration.

This is the operative tension flag: the universal-claim ("the
cosmos was imagined in the form of a gigantic tree" — singular
mode of being, cycle of inexhaustible regeneration) reads
Yggdrasil and the universal *aśvattha* and the gum-tree pole as
substrate-instances of the same structure. The Norwegian
substrate's reading of Yggdrasil — the tree gnawed at the roots
by the dragon, the tree whose damage is part of the cosmology
proper, the tree whose end *is* the cosmology — does not fit
Eliade's "endless regeneration" claim at full strength. This is
the sharpest Eliade-vs-Norse cosmology break the corpus carries.
Wave 4 will write the explicit tension; flag here as
`eliade-vs-yggdrasil-stable-vs-gnawed-axis`.

The tree-as-immortality-symbol extension at
[[eliade-sacred-and-profane#^p207]]: "The image of the tree was
not chosen only to symbolize the cosmos but also to express life,
youth, immortality, wisdom… myths of the quest for youth or
immortality give prominent place to a tree with golden fruit or
miraculous leaves, a tree growing 'in the distant land' and
guarded by monsters." The hero's confrontation with the guardian
monster, the gathering of fruit — initiatory ordeal of the heroic
type, with the tree as goal.

The vegetation-cults claim at
[[eliade-sacred-and-profane#^p208]]–[[eliade-sacred-and-profane#^p209]]:
"vegetation cults do not depend on a profane, 'naturistic'
experience, connected, for example, with spring and the
reawakening of vegetation. On the contrary, the religious
experience of renewal of the world precedes and justifies the
valorization of spring as the resurrection of nature. It is the
mystery of the periodical regeneration of the cosmos that is the
basis for the religious significance of spring." The cosmic tree
grounds the seasonal cycle, not the other way around.

The earlier axis-mundi mention of the tree at
[[eliade-sacred-and-profane#^p044]] (Numbakula's pole, the Achilpa
sacred pole "fashioned from the trunk of a gum tree") and at
[[eliade-sacred-and-profane#^p068]] ("the central post of the
house is assimilated to the Pillars of the World" — central pole
or post as world tree in primitive habitations) connects cosmic
tree to axis mundi: the tree-image is the most natural figure of
the connecting axis, especially in substrates where actual trees
are religiously load-bearing.

For the corpus: cosmic tree is a substrate-rich concept. Eliade
gives the universal claim; substrate-source reads will populate
the substrate variants — Yggdrasil at substrate scale, Bod the
Holy Oak in older Norwegian-Germanic substrate, the Christian
Cross-as-tree in syncretic forms. The Norwegian substrate's
relation to specific trees (the gardstre, the oak as Tor's tree,
the rowan as protective) extends the cosmic tree at substrate
scale; wave 3 fills.

## Norwegian substrate

### Confirmations

- Yggdrasil structurally confirms cosmic-tree at the substrate
  scale Eliade names at [[eliade-sacred-and-profane#^p207]] —
  three cosmic levels held by the tree, the tree as cosmos-
  living-organism, the tree as substrate-richest single image
  of cosmic regeneration. The structural shape Eliade reads is
  there.
- The gardstre at habitation scale confirms the cosmic-tree
  pattern at smaller scale — the central tree of the gard
  carries the tree-as-axis, tree-as-living-cosmos function at
  the household level.

### Breaks

- **Yggdrasil-as-gnawed breaks cosmic-tree as endless-regeneration
  sharply.** This is the sharpest single substrate-vs-universal
  break the corpus carries. Eliade's claim at
  [[eliade-sacred-and-profane#^p206]] is that the cosmic tree
  expresses "the mode of being of the cosmos, and first of all
  its capacity for endless regeneration." Norse Yggdrasil:
  - Niðhǫggr gnaws the root continuously
  - Four deer (Dáinn, Dvalinn, Duneyrr, Duraþrór) browse the
    crown
  - The eagle at the top and the dragon at the root pass
    enmity through Ratatoskr
  - The tree's destruction is part of Ragnarǫk's cosmology
    proper; the world-tree falls
  Yggdrasil is **gnawed-and-dying**, not endlessly-regenerating.
  Eliade's frame can describe the structural form (three levels,
  vertical connection) but cannot predict the
  stewardship-against-hostile-forces dimension or the
  eschatological-tree-fall.
- Flag for wave 4 as `eliade-vs-yggdrasil-stable-vs-gnawed-axis`.

### Augmentations

- The substrate augments via **stewardship-discipline as
  cosmological property of cosmic-tree**. The Norns water
  Yggdrasil from the well of Urðr; the cosmology *requires* the
  tree be tended. The cosmic-tree is not given-and-stable; it
  is held. This augments Eliade's frame with a *cosmological-
  labour* dimension the universal-claim does not carry.
- The substrate augments via the Iranian comparative parallel —
  the *gao-kərəna* tree gnawed by the lizard offers an IE-
  comparative companion to Yggdrasil's gnawing. The Norse and
  Iranian substrates together suggest a *gnawed-tree pattern*
  the broader IE substrate-set carries that Eliade's frame did
  not surface. See [[indo-european-substrate]] sphere
  touchpoint.
- The substrate augments via **substrate-specific tree-
  hierophanies at smaller scale**: the gardstre as household-
  axis, the rowan (rogn) as protective-tree against troldskap,
  the oak as Þórr's tree, the ash as tree-of-the-warrior. The
  cosmic-tree pattern at habitation scale carries substrate-
  specific operative properties Eliade's universal frame does
  not articulate.
