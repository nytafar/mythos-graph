# Postliminal

## Operational definition

Rites of incorporation into the new world, state, or social position.
The third phase of a rite of passage. Its function is to attach the
subject to the new condition publicly, materially, and with sufficient
weight that the previous condition cannot be casually re-entered.

Postliminal rites include shared meals, exchanges of gifts and blood,
formal entry through a door or portal, sprinkling with water or blood,
investiture with new clothing, the granting of a new name, the marking
of the body with permanent signs (tattoos, scars, removed teeth or
foreskin), and public recognition by the receiving community.

## Contributions

### From [[van-gennep-rites-of-passage]]

van Gennep coins the term at [[van-gennep-rites-of-passage#^p045]]:
"the ceremonies of incorporation into the new world, *postliminal
rites*." Catalogue of postliminal mechanisms appears across Ch III:
"actual contact (e.g., a slap, a handclasp), exchanging gifts of food
or valuables, eating, drinking, smoking a pipe together, sacrificing
animals, sprinkling water or blood, anointing, being attached to each
other, being covered together, or sitting on the same seat"
[[van-gennep-rites-of-passage#^p059]].

The shared meal is the paradigmatic postliminal rite — "a rite of
incorporation, of physical union" [[van-gennep-rites-of-passage#^p060]].
The exchange logic at [[van-gennep-rites-of-passage#^p061]],
[[van-gennep-rites-of-passage#^p062]] generalises: "to accept a gift is
to be bound to the giver" [[van-gennep-rites-of-passage#^p061]]. The
movement of objects creates the bond.

Mutilations — circumcision, tooth removal, scarification, ear piercing,
tattooing — are postliminal in their permanent-mark function: "the
mutilated individual is removed from the common mass of humanity by a
rite of separation… which automatically incorporates him into a defined
group; since the operation leaves ineradicable traces, the incorporation
is permanent" [[van-gennep-rites-of-passage#^p150]]. The mark fuses
preliminal and postliminal into a single physical act.

In Christian baptism, postliminal rites take the form of dressing in
white, being marked with the sign of the cross, admission to communion,
and the consecrated drink of milk and honey
[[van-gennep-rites-of-passage#^p194]] — the mythic structure of the
mysteries preserved in literate liturgy.

## Norwegian substrate

### Confirmations
- [[dugnad]] is the substrate-native postliminal form for task-defined
  micro-communities: the shared work *is* the rite of incorporation,
  with the closing kaffe og kaker / mat as the meal-in-common
  van Gennep names as paradigmatic postliminal at
  [[van-gennep-rites-of-passage#^p060]]. The work does what the meal
  does: it binds. The bond persists in the recognition that "we
  did this together."
- 17. mai performs national-postliminal annually: the parade, common
  meal (pølser, is, kake), russetog as the russefeiring's postliminal
  closure, and public recognition of full citizenship-status of those
  who ran russefeiring. Postliminal-of-postliminal nested per
  [[van-gennep-rites-of-passage#^p028]].
- *Konfirmasjonsdag* incorporates the konfirmant into the adult
  menighet through the laying-on-of-hands, the new-clothing
  (konfirmasjonsdrakt as the white-garment of
  [[van-gennep-rites-of-passage#^p194]]), the festmiddag, and the
  gifts that make the post-event social position visible.
- *Bryllupskaffe* / *bryllupsfest* is incorporation-weighted in
  substrate marriage practice — the wedding's rite-load sits
  postliminally, confirming van Gennep's weighting claim
  [[van-gennep-rites-of-passage#^p027]].

### Augmentations
- The substrate's *dugnad* extends postliminal-as-shared-meal to
  postliminal-as-shared-work. The work is the rite; the meal is the
  closure-of-the-rite, not the rite itself. van Gennep's frame
  privileges the meal as the unifier; the substrate shows the
  shared task can do the same work. Operative for brand-application:
  collaborative-effort as incorporation rite, not just shared-
  ceremony.
