# Illud tempus

## Operational definition

The mythical time of origins — *that time*, "in the beginning" — in
which the gods created the world and established the paradigmatic
acts. Not a historical past; not located on a chronological line.
Sacred time is reversible: the festival, the rite, the
commemoration *makes the illud tempus present again*. The
participant in the rite is no longer in their own historical time;
they are contemporary with the gods, with the cosmogony, with the
foundational act the rite reactualises.

Operative consequence: religious life is structured around
periodic return to the illud tempus. The festival is not
remembrance; it is reactualisation. Time itself is regenerated by
this return — the new year, the spring rite, the liturgical cycle
make sacred time available again, each iteration carrying the
same primordial weight.

## Contributions

### From [[eliade-sacred-and-profane]]

Eliade defines the term operationally at
[[eliade-sacred-and-profane#^p085]]: "by its very nature sacred
time is reversible in the sense that properly speaking it is a
primordial mythical time made present." The Latin tag *in illo
tempore* — "in that time" — appears throughout chapters II and III
as the standing reference for the founding instant. "Religious
participation in a festival implies emerging from ordinary
temporal duration and reintegration of the mythical time
reactualized by the festival itself." The crucial operative move:
the participant *meets* the illud tempus in the festival; they do
not merely remember it.

The Christianity-historicises move at
[[eliade-sacred-and-profane#^p089]]: "Christianity radically
changed the experience and the concept of liturgical time, and
this is due to the fact that Christianity affirms the historicity
of the person of Christ." The Christian liturgy "unfolds in a
historical time sanctified by the incarnation of the Son of God."
Pre-Christian sacred time is mythical-primordial; Christian
sacred time is historical-but-sanctified. The structure is
preserved (return to the illud tempus is still operative); the
content has shifted (the illud tempus is now Pontius Pilate's
Judaea rather than the gods' aboriginal acts).

The regeneration-through-return claim at
[[eliade-sacred-and-profane#^p101]]–[[eliade-sacred-and-profane#^p102]]:
"Through annual repetition of the cosmogony, time was regenerated,
that is, it began again as sacred time, for it coincided with the
*illud tempus* in which the world had first come into existence;
by participating ritually in the end of the world and in its
recreation, any man became contemporary with the *illud tempus*;
hence he was born anew." The operative claim: this "ritual
reactualizing of the *illud tempus* in which the first epiphany
of a reality occurred is the basis for all sacred calendars; the
festival is not merely the commemoration of a mythical event; it
reactualizes the event."

The succession-of-eternities formula at
[[eliade-sacred-and-profane#^p119]]: sacred time is "always the
same, that it is 'a succession of eternities.'" The participant
in this year's festival meets "the same sacred time the same that
had been manifested in the festival of the previous year or in
the festival of a century earlier." The illud tempus is not a
specific past instant repeated; it is the same instant, met again.
This is the strongest version of the reversibility claim.

The nostalgia-for-paradise extension at
[[eliade-sacred-and-profane#^p125]]–[[eliade-sacred-and-profane#^p126]]:
"the nostalgia for origins is equivalent to a religious
nostalgia. Man desires to recover the active presence of the
gods; he also desires to live in the world as it came from the
Creator's hands, fresh, pure, and strong." Eliade calls this
"in Christian terms… a nostalgia for paradise." See
[[nostalgia-for-paradise]] for the operative bridge to
customer-arc work.

The myth-as-paradigmatic-model claim at
[[eliade-sacred-and-profane#^p131]]–[[eliade-sacred-and-profane#^p132]]:
"The myth, then, is the history of what took place *in illo
tempore*, the recital of what the gods or the semidivine beings
did at the beginning of time… To tell a myth is to proclaim what
happened *ab origine*. Once told, that is, revealed, the myth
becomes apodictic truth." The illud tempus is not just a temporal
location; it is the source from which paradigms flow. See
[[paradigmatic-model]].

For the corpus: illud tempus is the operative term against the
modern brand-canon move that treats founding stories as biographical
or motivational. A brand origin story written as "this is what
happened in the founder's life" is biographical-historical; an
origin story written as "this is the founding instant the brand
returns to in every act" is illud-tempus-shaped. The customer
returning to the brand at each season, each new product, each
campaign is in the position of the festival participant: they are
not consuming new content, they are re-meeting the founding
instant. The customer-arc dimension reads against this concept.

The Norwegian substrate's calendar-density (jul, fastelavn, påske,
17. mai, midtsommer/jonsok, olsok) carries the illud-tempus
structure at high resolution; wave 3 will fill the substrate
section.

## Norwegian substrate

### Confirmations

- The Norwegian calendar carries illud-tempus structure at high
  density: jul reactualises midwinter renewal; fastelavn marks
  the entry into Lent's preliminal time; påske reactualises the
  Christian cosmogonic event (death-and-resurrection); jonsok /
  midtsommer reactualises the solar-cosmogonic moment; olsok
  (29. juli) reactualises Olav's martyrdom at Stiklestad; 17. mai
  reactualises 1814; the autumn rhythm of slakting and innhausting
  reactualises the substrate-agricultural founding-cycle. Each
  carries the festival-as-reactualisation discipline.

### Breaks

(none surfaced in this read at the level of structure — the
illud-tempus pattern holds; breaks live in eternal-return where
the *recoverability* is contested by Ragnarǫk)

### Augmentations

- The substrate augments via **bridging Eliade's pre-Christian /
  Christian rupture**. Eliade reads at
  [[eliade-sacred-and-profane#^p089]] that Christianity
  historicises sacred time — the illud tempus moves from the gods'
  aboriginal acts to Pontius Pilate's Judaea. The Norwegian
  substrate carries *both registers simultaneously*: olsok
  reactualises a historical-mythical event (Olav at Stiklestad,
  1030); jonsok reactualises a mythical-cosmological event (the
  solar founding); jul reactualises a syncretic both-at-once
  (the Christian Nativity layered onto pre-Christian midwinter
  renewal). The substrate runs Eliade's two registers in parallel
  rather than as historical sequence — augmentation Eliade's
  framing of the rupture cannot reach.
- The substrate augments via **calendar-density**. Eliade's
  examples are typically high-stakes single festivals (akitu,
  Nawroz). The Norwegian substrate carries the illud-tempus
  pattern at *every season* — the year is saturated with
  reactualisation occasions, not punctuated by them. Brand
  application inherits this: a Norwegian-substrate brand can
  inhabit a saturated sacred-calendar, not a sparse one.
