# Nested liminal

## Operational definition

The recursive structure that emerges when a transitional period is
sufficiently elaborated to acquire its own autonomy as a state. The
larger rite of passage retains its preliminal-liminal-postliminal
shape; inside the liminal phase, a second full sequence appears,
because entering the liminal state and leaving it are themselves
passages that require their own marking.

Nesting can recurse: betrothal as liminal between adolescence and
marriage carries its own internal preliminal-liminal-postliminal
sequence, and the entry into betrothal carries a still-finer nested
sequence. The schema is fractal where the culture treats the
intermediate states as substantively new conditions.

## Contributions

### From [[van-gennep-rites-of-passage|The Rites of Passage]]

van Gennep names the recursive principle at
[[van-gennep-rites-of-passage#^p028]]: "in certain ceremonial patterns
where the transitional period is sufficiently elaborated to constitute
an independent state, the arrangement is reduplicated. A betrothal
forms a liminal period between adolescence and marriage, but the
passage from adolescence to betrothal itself involves a special series
of rites of separation, a transition, and an incorporation into the
betrothed condition; and the passage from the transitional period,
which is betrothal, to marriage itself, is made through a series of
rites of separation from the former, followed by rites consisting of
transition, and rites of incorporation into marriage."

The autonomy claim is restated in the conclusions at
[[van-gennep-rites-of-passage#^p376]]: "the existence of transitional
periods which sometimes acquire a certain autonomy. Examples of these
are seen in the novitiate and the betrothal. It is this concept of
transition that provides an orientation for understanding the
intricacies and the order of rites preliminary to marriage."

The nested-liminal pattern explains why catalogue-of-rites approaches
that try to assign each rite to a single phase fail: many rites
participate in two phases simultaneously, because they straddle the
boundary of an outer ceremony and an inner one. The nested structure
is also why the book uses long ceremonial descriptions rather than
classified rite-lists; the order is only visible at the level of the
whole sequence, including the nested sub-sequences.

For the corpus, the nested-liminal is the principle that makes brand
customer-arc work coherent at multiple scales. Any beat in the larger
arc that genuinely changes the customer's state has its own nested
preliminal-liminal-postliminal structure inside it. Skipping the
nested rites, or collapsing the inner sequence into a single moment,
flattens the arc.

## Norwegian substrate

### Confirmations
- [[seterliv|Seterliv]] illustrates nested-liminal at clean substrate scale: the
  seter season is liminal *between* the agricultural cycle's home-
  phases, and within the seter season recurring sub-passages
  (saturday-bath, weekly church-walk down to the dal, harvest
  preparations) reduplicate preliminal/liminal/postliminal at
  finer grain. The recursion van Gennep names at
  [[van-gennep-rites-of-passage#^p028]] is visible at multiple scales
  in the substrate.
- [[konfirmasjon|Konfirmasjon]] preparation period nests inside the konfirmasjonstid
  which nests inside the broader childhood-to-adulthood arc. Three
  scales of liminal phasing visible in a single rite-cluster.
