# The stranger

## Operational definition

The figure who arrives at a community from outside it and whose
reception requires structured ritual mediation. The stranger is a
structural function (a position in the rite) before being a person —
the role is the same whether filled by an individual traveller, a
caravan, an ambassador, an adopted child, a slave, or a deity.

The stranger occupies a doubled state: weak (outside the community's
support) and strong (sacred relative to the community, not bound by
its profane laws, potentially carrying foreign power). This doubled
state is what reception rites address. The community must neither
absorb the stranger without ritual (which would erase the boundary)
nor refuse the stranger without rite (which would abandon hospitality
and miss the gift).

This concept is included as a *structural function* in the corpus.
It carries figure-like properties (recognisable, addressable as a
"who") while remaining a position rather than a personified
archetype. See framework-observations on whether structural-function
deserves a node type distinct from concept and archetype.

## Contributions

### From [[van-gennep-rites-of-passage|The Rites of Passage]]

van Gennep develops the stranger across Ch III as the paradigmatic
case of incorporation rites. The opening at
[[van-gennep-rites-of-passage#^p055]],
[[van-gennep-rites-of-passage#^p056]] establishes the doubled state:
"such a person is weak, because he is outside a given group or
society, but he is also strong, since he is in the sacred realm with
respect to the group's members, for whom their society constitutes
the secular world."

The reception sequence at
[[van-gennep-rites-of-passage#^p058]]: stop, wait, transitional
period, enter, be incorporated. This five-stage pattern is the
structure all subsequent incorporation rites in the book reproduce —
adoption, marriage, age-group ascent, ordination. The stranger is the
case from which the schema is read, parallel to how territorial
passage is the spatial paradigm.

Reception rites catalogued at
[[van-gennep-rites-of-passage#^p059]],
[[van-gennep-rites-of-passage#^p060]],
[[van-gennep-rites-of-passage#^p066]],
[[van-gennep-rites-of-passage#^p069]] include shared meals, exchange
of gifts, blood mixing, embracing, sitting together, communion. The
Masai goat-ear ceremony at [[van-gennep-rites-of-passage#^p066]] and
the Shammar Dakheel customs at [[van-gennep-rites-of-passage#^p069]]
illustrate the structural variability.

van Gennep notes the symmetry of arrival and leave-taking at
[[van-gennep-rites-of-passage#^p075]]. The stranger who has been
incorporated must be ritually un-incorporated to leave, and the
reverse ritual mirrors the original. The "perfect balance" between
arrival and departure rites is operative.

For the corpus, the stranger is the figure who informs every brand-
encounter beat that involves welcoming a new customer. The five-stage
reception (stop / wait / transition / enter / incorporate) is the
operative structure; collapsing it into a one-step "onboarding" is
the structural error.

## Norwegian substrate

### Confirmations
- The Norwegian substrate carries the doubled-state of the stranger in
  the customary distinction between fremmed (stranger as outsider) and
  gjest (stranger as ritually-incorporated guest). The fremmed→gjest
  passage is a structural rite of incorporation: arrival, halt outside,
  exchange of greetings, invitation across threshold, shared mat — the
  van Gennep five-step (stop / wait / transition / enter / incorporate)
  preserved.

### Breaks
- van Gennep's *magico-religious* framing of the stranger's doubled
  state (sacred and dangerous because mana-bearing) does not map cleanly
  onto Norwegian substrate. The substrate underwrites the stranger-
  relation through [[ætt-individuation|Ætt-individuation]] — the stranger is structurally
  outside the ætt-network, not magico-religiously charged. The
  hospitality obligations are real and binding, but their ground is
  ætt-loyalty extended to the visitor under guest-status, not the
  mana van Gennep posits across his Australian and Polynesian
  examples. The schema holds; the magico-religious explanatory
  layer breaks.
- *Fremmedfrykt* (substrate-native fear-of-strangers) is a separate
  ethical phenomenon from van Gennep's "magico-religious terror in
  his presence" attributed to Frazer/Crawley
  [[van-gennep-rites-of-passage#^p056]]. The substrate's fremmedfrykt
  is socially regulated (the stranger is dangerous-as-unknown, not
  dangerous-as-mana-bearer); the rites work at the social-regulation
  level without invoking the magico-religious. Tension flag for wave 4.

### Augmentations
- The substrate's gjestebud extends the stranger-incorporation past
  the rite proper. Once incorporated as gjest, the visitor enters
  long-term reciprocal obligation; the bond survives the visit and
  is invoked across years. van Gennep's frame describes the
  incorporation event; the substrate adds the durable status it
  produces.
