# Preliminal

## Operational definition

Rites of separation from a previous world, state, or social position.
The first phase of a rite of passage. Its function is to detach the
subject from the condition that must be left behind so that the
transitional state can be entered.

Preliminal rites include leave-takings, last meals, lustrations,
shavings, removal of ornaments, taboo impositions, departure
processions, declaration of a name change. Their common feature is
the breaking of a tie: with the home village, the previous age group,
the secular world, the living, the unmarried state, the asexual world.

## Contributions

### From [[van-gennep-rites-of-passage]]

van Gennep coins the term at [[van-gennep-rites-of-passage#^p045]]:
"rites of separation from a previous world, *preliminal rites*."

The territorial-passage paradigm of Ch II frames preliminal rites as
the spatial move out — leaving one's own land at the moment of
entering neutral territory [[van-gennep-rites-of-passage#^p041]]. The
same logic applies in non-spatial passages: separation from the asexual
world before initiation, separation from the patrician class before
adoption [[van-gennep-rites-of-passage#^p082]], separation from the
former household before marriage residence-change.

van Gennep notes that the symmetry of preliminal/postliminal rites is
visible in leave-taking sequences that mirror reception sequences:
visits, exchange of gifts, common meal, last drink, accompaniment on
the road [[van-gennep-rites-of-passage#^p075]]. The rite of arrival
performed in reverse becomes the rite of departure.

In some ceremonies the preliminal phase is reduced to a minimum — a
single act of consent or threshold-crossing — while in others it
extends across months, with seclusion, dietary taboos, and progressive
detachment. The variability is operative: the weighting of phases
encodes which transition the culture treats as costly.

## Norwegian substrate

### Confirmations
- [[konfirmasjon]] preparation phase is preliminal in canonical form:
  the konfirmant is removed from ordinary childhood patterns
  (scheduled instruction periods, prohibition on certain youthful
  activities during preparation in some bygder), with public
  recognition that the period is a separation-from-childhood.
- The seterferd departure (leaving the home gard for summer pasture,
  see [[seterliv]]) is preliminal-territorial in the substrate's
  agricultural rhythm.
- Norwegian funeral practice opens with kistelegging and salmer in the
  hjem before the prosesjon to the kirke — preliminal-from-the-living
  rites preserved in substrate-native form.

### Breaks
- (none structural)

### Augmentations
- The substrate carries *forlovelse* (engagement) as a publicly-marked
  preliminal stage between adolescence and marriage that enjoys more
  customary weight than van Gennep's compressed Western-European
  examples preserve. *Trolovelse* (older, customary betrothal) was
  itself a binding preliminal stage with legal effect; the layered
  forlovelse/trolovelse distinction is denser than van Gennep's frame
  reaches.
