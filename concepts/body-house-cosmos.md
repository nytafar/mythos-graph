# Body-house-cosmos

## Operational definition

The triple homology: the human body is a microcosm; the inhabited
house is a microcosm; the cosmos is the paradigm. Each is
structured by the same pattern — a centre, a vertical axis, an
opening above, a foundation below, the four directions, the
imitation of cosmogony. The three scales reproduce one another:
the spinal column is the cosmic pillar, the navel/heart is the
Centre of the World, the brahmarandhra (skull-opening) is the
roof-hole, the chimney is the body's exhalation made architectural.

Operative consequence: religious man inhabits all three scales
simultaneously and read each through the others. Physiological
acts are sacramental because the body is cosmically structured;
the house carries cosmological weight because it is structured
like the body and the cosmos; the cosmos is comprehensible because
it is structured like the body and the house. To desacralize one
scale is to desacralize all three; the closing of *The Sacred and
the Profane* states this explicitly.

## Contributions

### From [[eliade-sacred-and-profane]]

Eliade names the body-house-cosmos triad as the section heading
at [[eliade-sacred-and-profane#^p237]] and develops it through
[[eliade-sacred-and-profane#^p243]].

The first operative claim at
[[eliade-sacred-and-profane#^p074]] (chapter I) sets the
groundwork: "the cosmological meanings and ritual functions are
transferred to the chimney and to the part of the roof that lies
above the 'sacred area' and that is removed or even broken in
cases of prolonged death-agony. When we come to the homologation
cosmos-house-human body, we shall have occasion to show the deeper
meaning of breaking the roof."

The full formulation at
[[eliade-sacred-and-profane#^p237]]:

> Religious man lives in an open cosmos and… he is open to the
> world. This means that he is in communication with the gods;
> [and] that he shares in the sanctity of the world. That
> religious man can live only in an open world, we saw when we
> analyzed the structure of sacred space; man desires to dwell at
> a center, where there is the possibility of communicating with
> the gods. His dwelling is a microcosm; and so too is his body.
> The homology house-body-cosmos presents itself very early.

The Indian-religious-thought case at
[[eliade-sacred-and-profane#^p237]]: "The spinal column is
assimilated to the cosmic pillar or to Mount Meru; the breaths are
identified with the Winds; the navel or heart with the Center of
the World." The hatha-yoga text refers to the human body as "a
house with a pillar and nine doors." The body is read as imago
mundi at full development; the body is read as house at full
development; the three are the same structure at three scales.

The upper-opening claim immediately following: "each of these
equivalent images cosmos, house, human body displays, or is
capable of receiving, an upper opening that makes passage to
another world possible." The brahmarandhra at
[[eliade-sacred-and-profane#^p238]]: "the opening at the top of
the skull, which plays a primary role in yogico-tantric techniques
and through which the soul takes flight at the moment of death."
The custom of breaking the dead yogin's skull, the Indo-European
custom of removing roof boards during prolonged death-agony at
[[eliade-sacred-and-profane#^p239]] — each "facilitates the
departure of the soul" by opening the body or house's upper
seal. The body and the house both have a roof; the roof is what
the soul leaves through.

The homo-religiosus-as-microcosm extension at
[[eliade-sacred-and-profane#^p227]]–[[eliade-sacred-and-profane#^p230]]
(earlier in the chapter, before the explicit body-house-cosmos
section): the world "lives" and "speaks"; man "conceives of
himself as a microcosm. He forms part of the gods' creation; in
other words, he finds in himself the same sanctity that he
recognizes in the cosmos." The homology earth-woman: woman as
soil, seed as semen virile, agricultural work as conjugal union.
"Sow seed in her, ye men!" — Atharva Veda; "Your women are as
fields for you" — Koran.

The sanctification-of-life claim at
[[eliade-sacred-and-profane#^p231]]–[[eliade-sacred-and-profane#^p236]]:
"the whole of life is capable of being sanctified… life is lived
on a twofold plane; it takes its course as human existence and,
at the same time, shares in a transhuman life, that of the cosmos
or the gods." Eating as sacrament, sexual union as hierogamy,
conjugal life as anthropo-cosmic homology. See
[[sanctification-of-life]] for the full operative concept.

The Buddhist-Arhat extension at
[[eliade-sacred-and-profane#^p240]]–[[eliade-sacred-and-profane#^p241]]:
the Arhat who "breaks the roof of the house" and "flies through
the air" expresses ontological transcendence through the same
body-house-cosmos imagery — but now with the meaning shifted from
passage-from-human-to-superhuman to "abolition of the cosmos,
absolute freedom." The "shattering the roof" image is operative
across Indian philosophy and mysticism. Eliade is careful at
[[eliade-sacred-and-profane#^p242]]: "There is an immense
difference between the philosophical meaning of the Buddha's
broken egg or the roof shattered by the Arhats and the archaic
symbolism of passage from earth to heaven along the *axis mundi*
or through the smoke hole. Yet the fact remains that, among
symbols capable of expressing ontological breakthrough and
transcendence, both Indian philosophy and Indian mysticism chose
this primordial image of shattering the roof."

The closing diagnosis at
[[eliade-sacred-and-profane#^p243]] is the most-cited single
sentence in Eliade: "for the nonreligious men of the modern age,
the cosmos has become opaque, inert, mute; it transmits no
message, it holds no cipher." The body-house-cosmos triad has
collapsed: the body is no longer cosmologically structured (it is
biology), the house is no longer microcosm (it is "machine to
live in"), the cosmos is no longer cipher (it is matter). The
desacralization moves through all three scales simultaneously.

For the corpus: body-house-cosmos is the homology-of-scales that
makes Eliade's whole cosmology operative for ordinary life. The
brand workshop, the brand product, the brand workspace, the
embodied practice of the brand's people — all are scales of the
brand's cosmos, structured (in the operative case) by the same
pattern. The Norwegian substrate carries the homology in
tradition: tunet/kropp/kosmos at gard scale, ildsted/hjerte/sentrum
at hearth scale, røst/kropp/sjel at body-self scale. Wave 3 fills.

The closing-of-Eliade observation flagged for wave 4: the
body-house-cosmos closing diagnosis at
[[eliade-sacred-and-profane#^p243]] is the operative passage where
Eliade comes closest to Jung. Both name the same loss; Eliade
diagnoses the cosmological side, Jung the psychological side. This
is operative tension territory; flag as
`eliade-vs-jung-cosmology-vs-psyche`.

## Norwegian substrate

### Confirmations

- The substrate confirms body-house-cosmos at substrate scale at
  three nested levels: tunet / kropp / kosmos at gard scale
  (the gard's tun homologous to the body, the body homologous
  to the cosmos); ildsted / hjerte / sentrum at hearth scale
  (the hearth as the household's heart, the heart as the body's
  centre, the centre as cosmos-equivalent); røst / kropp / sjel
  at body-self scale (the voice / body / soul triad).
- The langhus's structural homology confirms the house-as-cosmos
  pattern Eliade names at [[eliade-sacred-and-profane#^p064]]–[[eliade-sacred-and-profane#^p068]]:
  the high-seat (sovereignty), the central hearth (axis-fire),
  the smoke-hole (upper opening) populate the four-part schema
  with substrate-native names.
- The substrate's saying-language carries the homology
  operatively: "kropp som hus" (body as house), "huset som
  kropp" (house as body) — the homology survives in idiom even
  where the cosmological frame has thinned.

### Augmentations

- The substrate augments via **substrate-native names for each
  scale**. The homology runs at gard scale (gard / kropp /
  kosmos), at langhus scale (the long-house's
  sovereignty / hearth / smoke-hole as body-and-cosmos
  inhabited at habitation scale), at hearth-room scale
  (ildsted / hjerte / sentrum). Each scale carries operative
  substrate-vocabulary; the brand-mythos inheritor can build
  on substrate-named structure rather than on Indian or
  Mediterranean-mediated terms.
- The substrate augments via the **gardstre / takbjelke / spinal
  column homology**: the central tree of the gard, the central
  beam of the langhus, the spinal column of the body all carry
  the same axis-mundi function at three scales. This extends
  Eliade's [[eliade-sacred-and-profane#^p237]] body-as-microcosm
  with substrate-named structural elements at habitation and
  landscape scale.
- The substrate augments via **the household-spirit (gardvord,
  nisse) as inhabitant of the homology**. Eliade's frame reads
  body-house-cosmos as structural; the substrate populates the
  homology with named spirit-figures that live across the
  body-house-cosmos triad — gardvord at gard / household scale,
  fylgja at body-and-soul scale (the body-and-fate-companion
  spirit). The homology is *inhabited*, not just structural.
