# Paradigmatic model

## Operational definition

The myth, the divine act, the founding gesture that establishes the
pattern every subsequent human act must imitate to be real. The
gods or culture heroes did *X* in illo tempore; men "do as the gods
did" — eat, marry, build, heal, fight, die — by reactualising the
paradigm. The paradigmatic model is the source of meaning for the
imitative act; the act is meaningful in the strict sense only as
imitatio dei.

Operative consequence: religious life is paradigmatic life. The
distinction between meaningful action and "vain and illusory
activity" is whether the act has a divine model it imitates. For
the brand-as-guide, the paradigmatic model is what the brand
*actually* offers the customer: not advice, not opinion, but the
revealed pattern the customer can live by to become the kind of
person the customer is becoming. The brand-as-guide names a
paradigm and embodies it; the customer, becoming hero, makes
themselves by imitating the paradigm.

## Contributions

### From [[eliade-sacred-and-profane]]

Eliade introduces the paradigmatic-model term across chapter II
sections "Regeneration through return to the Time of Origins" and
"Myth = Paradigmatic Model." The first operative claim at
[[eliade-sacred-and-profane#^p101]]–[[eliade-sacred-and-profane#^p103]]:
"Through annual repetition of the cosmogony, time was regenerated…
the cosmogony serves as the paradigmatic model for every
creation, for every kind of doing." The cosmogony is the
paradigm of paradigms; from it derive all other paradigms.

The festival-as-paradigm-reactualization at
[[eliade-sacred-and-profane#^p114]]–[[eliade-sacred-and-profane#^p118]]:
the Australian Arunta repeating the journey of the divine
Ancestor, the Polynesian Tikopia reproducing the "works of the
Gods." "Religious man periodically becomes the contemporary of
the gods in the measure in which he reactualizes the primordial
time in which the divine works were accomplished." The boat is
ritually repaired "not because it is in need of repair but because,
in illo tempore, the gods showed men how to repair boats. It is a
case not of an empirical operation but of a religious act, an
*imitatio dei*. The object repaired is no longer one of the many
objects that constitute the class 'boats' but a mythical archetype"
[[eliade-sacred-and-profane#^p118]].

The myth-as-paradigm definition at
[[eliade-sacred-and-profane#^p131]]–[[eliade-sacred-and-profane#^p134]]:

> The myth, then, is the history of what took place *in illo
> tempore*, the recital of what the gods or the semidivine beings
> did at the beginning of time… every creation springs from an
> abundance. The gods create out of an excess of power… This is
> why the myth, which narrates this sacred ontophany, this
> victorious manifestation of a plenitude of being, becomes the
> paradigmatic model for all human activities. For it alone reveals
> the real, the superabundant, the effectual. "We must do what the
> Gods did in the beginning," says an Indian text. "Thus the Gods
> did; thus men do."

The vain-action diagnosis at
[[eliade-sacred-and-profane#^p132]]: "what men do on their own
initiative, what they do without a mythical model, belongs to the
sphere of the profane; hence it is a vain and illusory activity,
and, in the last analysis, unreal. The more religious man is, the
more paradigmatic models does he possess to guide his attitudes
and actions. In other words, the more religious he is, the more
does he enter into the real and the less is he in danger of
becoming lost in actions that, being nonparadigmatic, 'subjective,'
are, finally, aberrant."

The captain-as-Aori case at
[[eliade-sacred-and-profane#^p135]]: the New Guinea sea captain
"personifies the mythical hero Aori. He wears the costume which
Aori is supposed to have worn… 'A man told me that when he went
fish shooting he pretended to be Kivavia himself.' He did not
*pray* to the mythical hero for aid and favor; he *identified
himself with him*." The paradigmatic model is not a moral
exemplar to admire; it is a pattern to inhabit.

The religious-man-makes-himself claim at
[[eliade-sacred-and-profane#^p141]]: "religious man assumes a
humanity that has a transhuman, transcendent model. He does not
consider himself to be truly man except in so far as he imitates
the gods, the culture heroes, or the mythical ancestors… Religious
man is not given; he makes himself, by approaching the divine
models." This is the operative bridge to customer-arc: the
customer, in Eliade's frame, becomes who they are by approaching
a paradigm. The brand-as-guide reveals the paradigm; the customer
makes themselves by imitating it.

The memory-and-the-true-sin claim at
[[eliade-sacred-and-profane#^p143]]–[[eliade-sacred-and-profane#^p144]]:
"The memory reactualized by the rites… plays a decisive role; what
happened *in illo tempore* must never be forgotten. The true sin
is forgetting." Memory of the paradigm is religious discipline;
forgetting is the failure that voids the imitative act. "It falls
to the primordial myth to preserve true history, the history of
the human condition; it is in the myth that the principles and
paradigms for all conduct must be sought and recovered."

For the corpus: paradigmatic model is operative for the
customer-arc dimension, the guide dimension, and the boon
dimension. The customer becomes a complete person by imitating
the paradigm the brand-as-guide reveals; the boon's identity
layer is the customer's having taken on the paradigm; the guide's
authority comes from being the keeper of the paradigm. A brand
that gives advice without a paradigm has not yet become guide. A
customer-arc that ends in benefit-language without identity-
transformation has not yet reached the paradigmatic layer.

The Norwegian substrate's paradigms (the bonde, the seterjente, the
draugen as cautionary anti-paradigm, Olav den hellige as
syncretic Christian-pagan paradigm) populate this concept at
substrate-native resolution; wave 3.

## Norwegian substrate

### Confirmations

- The substrate has paradigmatic-model structure at substrate
  scale: the bonde as agricultural-paradigm, the seterjente
  as seterliv-paradigm, Olav den hellige as syncretic
  Christian-pagan paradigm, the seid-volva as magico-religious
  paradigm. The "do as the gods (or saints, or ancestors) did"
  pattern operates.

### Breaks

- The substrate **breaks Eliade's imitatio-dei direction**. Where
  Eliade reads at [[eliade-sacred-and-profane#^p141]] that
  "religious man… makes himself, by approaching the divine
  models" — the gods, culture heroes, mythical ancestors —
  the Norwegian substrate's primary paradigm is **ancestor-
  imitation through ætt**, not god-imitation. The unit of
  paradigmatic-model is the *forebear-best-self carried in the
  lineage*, not the *divine template*. See [[ætt-individuation]].
- Operative consequence: the substrate runs paradigmatic-model
  *horizontally through time* (you imitate your forebears, who
  imitated theirs, in an unbroken ætt-line) rather than
  *vertically across ontological gap* (you imitate the gods,
  who exist on a different ontological plane). This is a
  cosmologically-loaded difference; the Norwegian-substrate
  brand inherits the horizontal pattern.
- Flag for wave 4 as `eliade-vs-norse-paradigm-imitatio-ancestor`.

### Augmentations

- The substrate augments via **ord (the binding word) as
  paradigmatic act**. The most operative paradigmatic act in
  the Norwegian substrate is not narrative-imitation of a
  founding deed; it is *the binding-word that holds across
  time*. The ord generates obligation-pattern that the ætt
  carries forward. This is paradigmatic-model in
  speech-act register Eliade's frame does not articulate.
- The substrate augments via **the brand-side operative
  implication**: customer-paradigm in
  Norwegian-substrate brand-mythos is "your forebear-best-self"
  not "your divine-template-self." The customer's becoming is
  return-to-lineage rather than approximation-to-god. This is
  operative for [[customer-arc]] dimension when the brand
  works in Norwegian-substrate register.
