# Allemannsretten

## Operational definition

Norwegian customary right of passage and stay in the landscape.
Codified in friluftsloven (1957). Confers partial-liminal status on
the wanderer-on-foot: passage is permitted across uncultivated land,
with threshold restrictions encoded as the 150 m rule (no closer
than 150 metres to inhabited buildings).

Substrate-native legal-customary expression of van Gennep's
pivoting-of-sacred: the wanderer is in-the-territory-but-not-of-it,
their status pivoting as they cross from utmark to innmark. The
150 m threshold is a codified neutral zone in van Gennep's
territorial-passage sense.

Cross-tradition note on [[sacred-space]]: allemannsretten is the
substrate's operative claim that **sacred-space-as-shared-resource**
is a substrate principle Eliade's individual-encounter framing at
[[eliade-sacred-and-profane#^p018]]–[[eliade-sacred-and-profane#^p082]]
underdescribes. The landscape carries hierophanic charge; access
to that charge is *legally and customarily distributed* rather
than priestly-mediated. The Norwegian substrate has no
temple-cult monopoly on sacred-space; the customary access-pattern
*is* the cosmology's operative claim about how sacred-space
belongs.
