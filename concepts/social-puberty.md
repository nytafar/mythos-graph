# Social puberty

## Operational definition

The socially marked transition from childhood to adulthood, distinct
from physiological puberty (menarche, sperm emission, secondary sexual
characteristics) with which it does not reliably coincide. Social
puberty is the position the society treats as adult; physiological
puberty is the body's biological readiness for reproduction. The two
diverge across cultures, classes, and individuals.

Operative consequence: initiation rites are not "puberty rites." They
mark and produce social adulthood, which has its own timing,
prerequisites, and content. To collapse the social transition into the
physiological event is to mistake the regulated act for the unregulated
condition.

## Contributions

### From [[van-gennep-rites-of-passage]]

van Gennep introduces the distinction at
[[van-gennep-rites-of-passage#^p140]] and develops it across Ch VI as
the corrective to Schurtz's and Webster's framing of initiation as
puberty rite [[van-gennep-rites-of-passage#^p138]],
[[van-gennep-rites-of-passage#^p141]],
[[van-gennep-rites-of-passage#^p144]].

The empirical case: physiological puberty varies enormously across
populations and individuals. In Rome, "girls are legally marriageable
at the age of twelve, but only a twelfth of the Roman girls menstruate
at that age." In Paris, "it is legal to be married at sixteen years
and six months, but the average age at puberty is fourteen years and
four months" [[van-gennep-rites-of-passage#^p140]]. *Social puberty
precedes physiological puberty in Rome and follows it in Paris.* The
dates do not align; therefore one of them is not what the rites are
about.

The Toda case at [[van-gennep-rites-of-passage#^p148]] shows the
extreme: ritualised pre-pubertal sexual encounters with no marriage
following until years after physiological puberty. The Masai case at
[[van-gennep-rites-of-passage#^p176]] shows the same — circumcision
postponed for poverty until parents can pay the cost.

van Gennep generalises: "the physical puberty of girls is marked by…
above all the first menstrual flow. Therefore it would seem simple to
date the transition from childhood to adolescence from the first
appearance of these signs. But from a social standpoint this is not
the case" [[van-gennep-rites-of-passage#^p140]]. The body's calendar
is not the society's calendar.

For the corpus, the operative consequence runs in both directions.
Marketing applications that frame brand-onboarding as a "natural
moment" — first purchase, first signup, first usage — anchor the rite
to a physiological-equivalent (the body's act) rather than a social-
puberty-equivalent (the community's recognition that the new person
exists). The first signup is not the threshold; the threshold is when
the community treats the person as initiated. See
[[initiation-as-puberty-rite]].

## Norwegian substrate

### Confirmations
- [[konfirmasjon]] is the substrate's clearest instance of van Gennep's
  social-puberty distinction. Celebrated at 14–15 regardless of the
  konfirmant's physiological development (kept stable across
  generations even as physical menarche has moved earlier). The
  schedule is social, not biological. The rite produces an adulthood-
  status (the konfirmant is now allowed to take communion, which marks
  full menighetsmedlemskap) that is decoupled from physical maturation.
- [[russefeiring]] reinforces the same distinction at the secular-
  modern register: the russetid runs from the spring of avgangsår
  (typically age 18–19), structured around the social passage of
  fullført videregående, not around puberty (which has been complete
  for years). Social-puberty as graduation-ritual.

### Breaks
- (none structural)

### Augmentations
- The substrate's konfirmasjon retains both halves of the original
  distinction more cleanly than secularised modern equivalents
  elsewhere — it is publicly social-puberty (community recognition
  of adulthood-readiness) without claiming to be physiological
  (no one pretends the 14-year-old is biologically transitioning at
  the rite). Useful as substrate-anchor when distinguishing
  social-puberty from physical puberty in brand contexts —
  the rite's legibility-as-social is preserved.
