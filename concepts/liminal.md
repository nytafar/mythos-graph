# Liminal

## Operational definition

Of or pertaining to the threshold. In van Gennep's schema, the rites
of transition that constitute the middle phase of a rite of passage —
the period when the subject is no longer in their previous state but
has not yet entered the new one. The liminal state is hereinafter
called the threshold state: between, suspended, sacred-by-virtue-of-
not-belonging.

Liminal rites mark and regulate this between-condition. They include
seclusion, ritual death, the wearing of special dress or marks, the
suspension of ordinary speech and food, instruction in tribal lore,
the display of sacra, simulated rebirth, and physical mutilation. The
condition itself can be brief (the moment on the threshold) or long
(the years of novitiate, the months of betrothal, the period between
death and final funeral).

The liminal phase is the source of the schema's generative power.
When a liminal period acquires autonomy — when "between" becomes its
own state with its own rites — the entire pattern reduplicates inside
the original sequence (see [[nested-liminal]]).

## Contributions

### From [[van-gennep-rites-of-passage]]

van Gennep names the term at [[van-gennep-rites-of-passage#^p045]]:
"those executed during the transitional stage *liminal (or threshold)
rites*." The term derives from Latin *limen* (threshold).

The territorial paradigm gives the liminal its concrete shape: the
neutral zone between two territories, where the traveller "wavers
between two worlds" [[van-gennep-rites-of-passage#^p039]]. This is
not a metaphor for the liminal condition; the condition is materialised
in space first, then translated to other passages.

Liminal rites in initiation are catalogued across Ch VI: the novice
secluded in the bush [[van-gennep-rites-of-passage#^p155]], considered
dead and later resurrected [[van-gennep-rites-of-passage#^p158]],
subjected to flagellation, taboos on food and speech, intoxication, and
the unveiling of the sacra [[van-gennep-rites-of-passage#^p168]],
[[van-gennep-rites-of-passage#^p164]]. The Eleusinian and Christian
parallels at [[van-gennep-rites-of-passage#^p186]],
[[van-gennep-rites-of-passage#^p193]] show the same pattern preserved
in literate-religious form.

van Gennep's claim that liminal periods can become autonomous
[[van-gennep-rites-of-passage#^p376]] is the schema's recursive
engine. Betrothal is liminal between adolescence and marriage, and
within betrothal the pattern reduplicates: separation from
adolescence, betrothal as transition, incorporation into the
betrothed condition; then separation from betrothal, marriage as
transition, incorporation into the married state
[[van-gennep-rites-of-passage#^p028]].

The liminal subject is sacred relative to the secular world they have
left and the secular world they are about to enter — see
[[pivoting-of-sacred]] for the mechanism.

## Norwegian substrate

### Confirmations
- [[russefeiring]] is the substrate's clearest contemporary instance
  of an autonomous liminal phase per
  [[van-gennep-rites-of-passage#^p376]]. Russetiden (Easter to 17.
  mai) carries every diagnostic property of van Gennep's liminal:
  visible mark (red overall, comparable in function to the white-clay
  of the Australian initiate at
  [[van-gennep-rites-of-passage#^p177]]), suspended ordinary rules
  (sosial omveltning institutionalised), special speech and
  practices (russenavn, russekort, russerop), public recognition of
  the suspended-state, and ritual closure at 17. mai with national
  reincorporation.
- [[seterliv]] is the seasonal liminal in agricultural form: the seter
  is the liminal *zone* (territorial), the seterperiod is the liminal
  *time* (calendrical). Both inflect with the same structure;
  van Gennep's recursive-liminal claim
  [[van-gennep-rites-of-passage#^p028]] is illustrated by the
  substrate doubling.
- Konfirmasjonstid is liminal in Lutheran-substrate form: the
  konfirmant is publicly between-states (no longer barn, not yet
  adult-in-the-menighet), with weekly katekese as the suspended-rules
  instruction phase.

### Augmentations
- The substrate's *seterliv* augments van Gennep's liminal-as-time-or-
  zone with liminal-as-economy. The seter is not just a sacred-by-
  separation place; it is a productive economy operating under
  different customary rules (women as primary actors, separate
  property and labour conventions). The liminal here is functional,
  not just symbolic. van Gennep's frame can describe the time and the
  zone; the substrate adds the productive-economy layer.
- *Husmannsånd* and substrate egalitarian sensibility produce a
  liminal-as-equaliser strain (russefeiring's class-mixing, dugnad's
  rank-suspension) that Eliade-style sacred-as-set-apart frames do
  not predict. Liminal as social-equaliser is operative in the
  substrate.
