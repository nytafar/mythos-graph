# Seterliv

## Operational definition

Norwegian seasonal pastoral practice — the summer move of livestock
and (historically) primarily women from the home gard to mountain
pasture (seter). Structurally a seasonal liminal: preliminal-
territorial in the seterferd departure, liminal-zone in the seter
itself (suspended from ordinary social order, governed by its own
customary rules), postliminal in the heimferd at autumn.

Substrate-native instance of nested-liminal: the seter season is
liminal between home-cycles, and within the season recurring sub-
passages (saturday-bath, weekly church-walk, harvest preparations)
reduplicate the structure at finer grain.

The seter is also a productive economy with its own customary rules
(women as primary actors; separate property and labour conventions),
which augments van Gennep's liminal-as-time-or-zone with
liminal-as-economy. See substrate section in [[liminal|Liminal]] and
[[territorial-passage|Territorial passage]].
