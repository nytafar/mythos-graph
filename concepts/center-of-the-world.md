# Center of the World

## Operational definition

The located point at which the cosmic axis meets the inhabited
world. Distinct from [[axis-mundi]] (the connecting structure
running through the three cosmic zones) and from [[imago-mundi]]
(the cosmic image at scale): the Center of the World is the
specific point where the axis stands and where the world
proliferates outward. The "navel of the earth," the "highest
point," the place where heaven and earth and underworld meet.

Operative consequence: the brand's centre is the located point
where its axis stands. Not a metaphor — a concrete claim about
where the cosmology originates and where the brand's most
load-bearing acts are performed. A brand without a Centre of the
World has not yet located its axis; a brand with too many centres
has not held the discipline of identifying *the* one.

## Contributions

### From [[eliade-sacred-and-profane]]

Eliade names the Center of the World as element (d) of the system-
of-the-world schema at
[[eliade-sacred-and-profane#^p045]]: "around this cosmic axis
lies the world, hence the axis is located 'in the middle,' at the
'navel of the earth'; it is the Center of the World." The Centre
is the consequence of the axis: wherever the axis stands, the
world spreads out around it, and the standing-place is the
Centre.

The cosmic-mountain case at
[[eliade-sacred-and-profane#^p047]]: Meru, Haraberezaiti, the
Mount of the Lands, Gerizim — "since the sacred mountain is an
*axis mundi* connecting earth with heaven, it in a sense touches
heaven and hence marks the highest point in the world;
consequently the territory that surrounds it… is held to be the
highest among countries." Each substrate names its own Centre;
the Centre is always *here*, and "here" is wherever the substrate
locates the founding axis. The Christian Golgotha "is on the
summit of the cosmic mountain"; the Islamic Ka'ba "faces the
center of Heaven"; the Hebrew Holy Land "was not submerged by the
Flood" because it is highest.

The proliferation-of-centres claim at
[[eliade-sacred-and-profane#^p052]]–[[eliade-sacred-and-profane#^p053]]:
"The multiplicity, or even the infinity, of centers of the world
raises no difficulty for religious thought. For it is not a
matter of geometrical space, but of an existential and sacred
space that has an entirely different structure, that admits of an
infinite number of breaks and hence is capable of an infinite
number of communications with the transcendent." A village can
be a Centre, the city above it can be a Centre, the temple inside
the city can be a Centre, the holy of holies inside the temple
can be a Centre. The Centre is not unique by exclusion; it is
unique by participation in the same vertical structure.

The Achilpa carrying the sacred pole at
[[eliade-sacred-and-profane#^p053]]: the Achilpa "always carried
the sacred pole, the *axis mundi*, with them, so that they should
never be far from the Center and should remain in communication
with the supraterrestrial world." Even nomadic peoples need the
Centre; the Centre travels with them in the carried pole. The
Centre is wherever the inhabited world's axis is currently
standing, not a fixed geographical location.

The ontological-thirst claim at
[[eliade-sacred-and-profane#^p081]]: "religious man's will to take
his stand at the very heart of the real, at the Center of the
World, that is exactly where the cosmos came into existence and
began to spread out toward the four horizons, and where, too,
there is the possibility of communication with the gods; in
short, precisely where he is closest to the gods." The Centre is
not a geographic preference; it is the location where being is
most concentrated, where the founding instant is closest, where
the gods are nearest. The desire to be at the Centre is the
desire to be at the source.

For the corpus: Centre of the World is the operative concept for
locating the brand's primary site — the workshop, the home
office, the founding location, the flagship product, the canonical
event. The discipline distinguishes the Centre from the merely
busy location: the Centre is where the cosmogony is closest, not
where the most activity happens. The Norwegian substrate carries
the Centre at multiple scales — the gard's tun, the village's
kirke, the country's Stortinget, the home's ildsted — wave 3
fills.

## Norwegian substrate

### Confirmations

- The substrate confirms the proliferation-of-centres principle
  Eliade names at
  [[eliade-sacred-and-profane#^p052]]–[[eliade-sacred-and-profane#^p053]].
  Norse cosmology carries multiple Centres: Ásgarðr (the
  cosmic-divine Centre at the world tree), Miðgarðr (the
  human-cosmic Centre), the gard's tun (the household-scale
  Centre), the ildsted (the hearth-scale Centre). Each is
  Centre at its own scale.
- Stiklestad as historical-substrate Centre (the place where
  Olav fell, the kingdom's hierophanic anchor); the Stortinget
  as civic-modern Centre; Akershus as historical-political
  Centre. The substrate runs the proliferation across registers
  (cosmic, household, historical, civic).

### Breaks

(none surfaced in this read at structural level; the substrate's
*hierarchical* relation between centres is augmentation, not break)

### Augmentations

- The substrate augments via **hierarchical asymmetry between
  the proliferating centres**. Eliade's principle predicts
  proliferation-without-rank ("multiplicity, or even infinity,
  of centers… raises no difficulty"). The Norwegian substrate's
  Ásgarðr / Miðgarðr / Útgarðr arrangement is *hierarchical and
  morally weighted* — Ásgarðr is sacred-saturated, Miðgarðr
  human-stewarded, Útgarðr peripheral-troldsk. The centres are
  not equally sacred; they are ranked by ontological
  concentration. This augments Eliade's frame with a *gradient*
  the universal-claim does not predict.
- The substrate augments via **gard-scale operative Centre**.
  The gard's tun is the household-scale Centre at full
  cosmological weight — the place where the gardstre stands,
  the åseterett's anchor, the location where weddings, births,
  funerals, dugnad-meals, jul-fester all happen. The brand-mythos
  inheritor: a Norwegian-substrate brand has a *located*
  operative Centre (workshop, founding location), not a
  symbolic-only one.
- The substrate augments via **Stiklestad-as-syncretic-Centre**.
  The Centre where pre-Christian and Christian Norway meet at
  the conversion-event; the place is sacred *both* as historical-
  martyr-site and as substrate-pagan-battlefield. The substrate
  runs syncretic centres Eliade's frame predicts via the
  proliferation principle but does not articulate operatively.
