# Sacra

## Operational definition

The sacred objects displayed to the initiate at the culmination of
initiation: masks, bull-roarers, sacred ears of corn, churinga, the
contents of the kista or cista mystica. The sacra are what the
neophyte is shown — and the showing is the central event around which
the rest of the initiation organises.

The sacra carry weight not because the objects are intrinsically
powerful but because their concealment from the uninitiated is the
condition of the social order the initiate is entering. To see the
sacra is to know what only members know; to manipulate the sacra is
to be authorised to act in the role.

## Contributions

### From [[van-gennep-rites-of-passage|The Rites of Passage]]

van Gennep names the centrality at
[[van-gennep-rites-of-passage#^p164]]: "in North America, as in
Australia, the sacra are unveiled to the novices; they are revealed
respectively as masks and bull-roarers… The exposing of these bogymen
of their childhood is the central event of the ritual. Furthermore,
it is the privilege of the initiated to manipulate the sacra
according to precise rules without danger from the supernatural.
These elements also constitute the culminating points of initiation
into Asian and Greek mysteries."

van Gennep cites Harrison's definition at
[[van-gennep-rites-of-passage#^p185]]: mysteries are "a rite at which
are exhibited certain *sacra* which may not be seen by the worshipper
if he has not undergone a certain purification." The display of sacra
at Eleusis is the culminating rite, but the display alone does not
constitute the mysteries — the entire sequence (preliminal, liminal,
postliminal) does.

The Tubuan unmasking in the Bismarck Archipelago at
[[van-gennep-rites-of-passage#^p170]]: "The *tubuan* undresses
completely, so that the novice recognizes that he is a man. The
*tubuan's* clothing stands alone because it is armored, proving that
it is imbued with power." The discovery is double — the figure of
terror is revealed as a man, and the clothing alone retains the
mana. Knowledge of the sacra reorganises the relationship between
visible object and invisible power.

Operative property: the sacra mark the boundary between insider and
outsider knowledge. The exposure to the sacra is the moment the
novice acquires authority to act in a role they previously occupied
only as recipient of mystery.

## Norwegian substrate

### Confirmations
- The substrate's *kirke-sølv* (silver chalice, paten), *altartavle*,
  and the *konfirmasjonsbibel* function as sacra in van Gennep's
  sense — objects whose display and handling is restricted to
  ritually-prepared persons, whose right to handle them is itself a
  postliminal mark of incorporation. The konfirmasjonsbibel as a
  personal sacrum given at konfirmasjonsdag confirms van Gennep's
  unveiling-of-the-sacra structure
  [[van-gennep-rites-of-passage#^p164]] in late-modern Norwegian form.

### Augmentations
- Substrate folk-sacra (the bunad's silver, the heirloom *bolle*
  passed at bryllup, the husaltre with its ancestral images) operate
  at finer-grained domestic scale than van Gennep's catalogue
  (Eleusinian sacra, Catholic chalice, Australian churinga). The
  substrate distributes sacra-discipline across the home as well as
  the temple. Useful for brand-application: not all sacra need to be
  liturgical-grade; the customary domestic sacrum carries comparable
  weight at smaller scope.
