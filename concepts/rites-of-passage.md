# Rites of passage

## Operational definition

The ceremonial pattern that accompanies any passage from one social or
cosmic state to another. Its structure is tripartite: rites of separation
(preliminal), rites of transition (liminal), rites of incorporation
(postliminal). The pattern recurs across birth, initiation, betrothal,
marriage, funerals, seasonal change, territorial crossing, and ordination
— under varied forms and with varied weighting of phases, but with the
same underlying order.

The unit of meaning is the order, not the rite. Individual rites
extracted from sequence lose what makes them rites of passage.

## Contributions

### From [[van-gennep-rites-of-passage]]

van Gennep names the schema at [[van-gennep-rites-of-passage#^p027]]:
"these three subcategories are not developed to the same extent by all
peoples or in every ceremonial pattern. Rites of separation are prominent
in funeral ceremonies, rites of incorporation at marriages. Transition
rites may play an important part…"

The book's argument is that a vast catalogue of ceremonies — pregnancy,
childbirth, naming, puberty, betrothal, marriage, ordination, funerals,
territorial crossing, vendetta, adoption, seasonal festivals — share one
underlying order. The unifying claim is structural, not thematic: the
rites do not resemble each other because they share content, but because
they share function: to move a person or group from one defined position
to another defined position, with the in-between treated as its own
condition that must be regulated.

Three load-bearing assertions sit at [[van-gennep-rites-of-passage#^p375]],
[[van-gennep-rites-of-passage#^p376]], [[van-gennep-rites-of-passage#^p377]]:

1. The pattern is generic. The differences across cultures lie in detail
   and weighting, not in the underlying arrangement.
2. Transitional periods can acquire autonomy — betrothal between
   adolescence and marriage, the novitiate before initiation, the
   "transitory stage" of the dead between death and final rest.
   Liminal autonomy is the key generative principle that produces
   nested rites of passage.
3. Social passage is identified with territorial passage. The crossing
   of the threshold, the entry through the portal, the movement across
   the neutral zone — these are not metaphors. The passage is enacted
   spatially because for the participants the spatial act *is* the
   social act.

van Gennep's discipline against Frazer, Crawley, Hartland, and the
folklorists of his time is the order-not-rite move: the meaning of any
specific rite emerges from its position within a sequence
[[van-gennep-rites-of-passage#^p184]]. Extract the rite from the
sequence and you have a curiosity, not a phenomenon.

The cyclical case appears at [[van-gennep-rites-of-passage#^p381]]:
among the Lushae, rites of passage form a closed loop from life to
death and death back to life. van Gennep notes Buddhism's ethical
extension and Nietzsche's psychological extension of the same shape.

## Norwegian substrate

### Confirmations
- [[konfirmasjon]] confirms the schema in late-modern Norwegian Lutheran
  form: a year-long preparation phase (preliminal — separation from
  childhood through scheduled removal from ordinary play and entry into
  catechetical instruction), the konfirmasjonstime as liminal sacred-
  time, and the konfirmasjonsdag itself as postliminal incorporation
  into the adult community.
- [[bryllup]] confirms van Gennep's incorporation-weighting of marriage
  rites: the brudeferd procession, gavebord, and bryllupskaffe all sit
  in the postliminal phase; the wedding does its work after the vows.
- [[gravferd]] confirms van Gennep's separation-weighting of funeral
  rites: kistelegging, salmer, and the ride from kirke to grav stage
  the separation phase as the load-bearing one, with the
  [[minnestund]] performing a compressed but real postliminal
  reincorporation of the surviving community.
- [[russefeiring]] is a substrate-native instance of an autonomous
  liminal phase per [[van-gennep-rites-of-passage#^p376]]: the russetid
  (roughly Easter to 17. mai) is structurally a liminal period with its
  own internal preliminal/liminal/postliminal arc — separation from
  ordinary studenthood at russedåp, the tid of suspended-rules with
  visible liminal mark (red overall), and incorporation at russedåp's
  closure on 17. mai national reincorporation.

### Breaks
- (none structural — the schema's order-as-meaning thesis holds against
  Norwegian substrate at every life-stage tested)

### Augmentations
- The Norwegian rite-corpus carries a denser civic-liturgical layer
  than van Gennep's examples (russefeiring around national-day
  reincorporation, dugnad as substrate-native incorporation rite for
  task-defined micro-communities). The schema absorbs these without
  modification, but the corpus they extend is richer than the
  comparative-ethnography sample van Gennep worked from. See
  [[dugnad]], [[seterliv]], [[allemannsretten]] for adjacent
  substrate-instances.
