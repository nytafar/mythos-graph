# Cosmogony

## Operational definition

The creation of the world by the gods, narrated in myth and
reactualised in rite. The cosmogony is the founding instant — the
slaying of the primordial dragon, the fashioning of the cosmos
from chaos, the Word spoken over the waters — and it is the
*paradigm of every subsequent creation*. Settling a territory,
building a house, raising an altar, healing a body, installing a
sovereign, being born: each is read as a repetition of the
cosmogony at smaller scale.

Operative consequence: every meaningful creation must imitate the
gods' creation. The brand's founding is not a one-time biographical
event but a cosmogony in operative sense — the founding instant
that every subsequent act of brand-creation (new product, new hire,
new partnership) repeats and is measured against. A brand whose
products do not echo its founding cosmogony is making non-
paradigmatic acts in Eliade's sense — "vain and illusory activity,
and, in the last analysis, unreal"
[[eliade-sacred-and-profane#^p132]].

## Contributions

### From [[eliade-sacred-and-profane]]

Eliade introduces cosmogony as a load-bearing term across
chapters I–II of *The Sacred and the Profane*. The first
operative claim at
[[eliade-sacred-and-profane#^p020]]: "The manifestation of the
sacred ontologically founds the world." Hierophany and cosmogony
are linked: every hierophany founds a world; every world-founding
is cosmogonic.

The settling-as-cosmogony claim at
[[eliade-sacred-and-profane#^p036]]–[[eliade-sacred-and-profane#^p038]]:
"to organize a space is to repeat the paradigmatic work of the
gods." The Vedic ritual for taking possession of a territory —
erection of a fire altar to Agni — is "nothing but the
reproduction on the microcosmic scale of the Creation." The
Scandinavian colonisation of Iceland is read in the same frame:
"their labor was only repetition of a primordial act, the
transformation of chaos into cosmos by the divine act of
creation." The Spanish and Portuguese conquistadores raising the
Cross to consecrate new territory at
[[eliade-sacred-and-profane#^p039]] are the late-modern Christian
inheritor of the same pattern.

The building-sacrifice case at
[[eliade-sacred-and-profane#^p070]]–[[eliade-sacred-and-profane#^p073]]:
"the cosmogonic myth explains the Creation through the slaying of
a giant; his organs give birth to the various cosmic regions… It
is on this type of cosmogonic myth that building sacrifices
depend." The Indian foundation rite — driving a stake through the
snake's head before laying the cornerstone — repeats Indra's
slaying of the cosmic snake in the Rig Veda. The southeastern
European ballads of the master mason's wife immured in the
foundation are the same pattern in folk-Christian register.

The annual cosmogony at
[[eliade-sacred-and-profane#^p096]]–[[eliade-sacred-and-profane#^p100]]:
the Babylonian akitu festival, with its solemn recitation of the
*Enuma elish* in the last days of the old year and the first days
of the new, "reactualized the combat between Marduk and the
marine monster Tiamat." The cosmogony is performed each year. The
new year is not chronological succession but a return to the
founding instant — "the world that had existed for a whole year
really disappeared," and Marduk had to create it again. The
Persian Nawroz at p099, the New Year purifications, the
Saturnalia-style social confusion preceding renewal — each is a
ritual recapitulation of cosmogony.

The cosmogony-as-paradigmatic-model claim at
[[eliade-sacred-and-profane#^p133]]: "every creation is a divine
work and hence an irruption of the sacred… every creation springs
from an abundance. The gods create out of an excess of power, an
overflow of energy is accomplished by a surplus of ontological
substance. This is why the myth, which narrates this sacred
ontophany… becomes the paradigmatic model for all human
activities." See [[paradigmatic-model]] for the operative
extension.

The healing-as-cosmogony case at
[[eliade-sacred-and-profane#^p103]]–[[eliade-sacred-and-profane#^p105]]:
the Polynesian use of cosmogonic words to heal, to fecundate, to
prepare for war; the Na-khi therapeutic ritual that recites the
creation of the world before naming any specific medicine. "Life
cannot be repaired, it can only be recreated through symbolic
repetition of the cosmogony, for… the cosmogony is the paradigmatic
model for all creation." This is the strongest version: any real
creation, including healing, is cosmogonic in form.

The recapitulation summary at
[[eliade-sacred-and-profane#^p149]]: "At each New Year the
cosmogony is reiterated, the world recreated, and to do this is
also to create time that is, to regenerate it by beginning it
anew. This is why the cosmogonic myth serves as paradigmatic model
for every creation or construction; it is even used as a ritual
means of healing."

The initiation-as-cosmogony case at
[[eliade-sacred-and-profane#^p262]]: "Initiatory death reiterates
the paradigmatic return to chaos, in order to make possible a
repetition of the cosmogony — that is, to prepare the new birth.
… Man constantly reperforms the cosmogony — the paradigmatic
making — in order to be sure that he is making something well — a
child, for example, or a house, or a spiritual vocation. This is
why rites of initiation always present a cosmogonic valence." The
personal scale of the cosmogony pattern; see [[death-rebirth]].

For the corpus: cosmogony is the structural concept that connects
the brand's founding instant ([[origin]] dimension) with every
subsequent creative act (new product, new hire, new campaign).
The discipline: each creative act repeats the founding cosmogony,
or it is non-paradigmatic and therefore unreal in Eliade's frame.
The Norwegian substrate's cosmogonies (the Norse creation from
Ymir's body, the gard founding rites, jul as midwinter renewal)
populate this concept at substrate-native scale; wave 3 fills.

## Norwegian substrate

### Confirmations

- Norse cosmogony confirms Eliade's dismemberment-cosmogony pattern
  ([[eliade-sacred-and-profane#^p070]]–[[eliade-sacred-and-profane#^p073]]).
  Ymir (the primordial giant) is slain by Óðinn, Vili, and Vé;
  his body becomes the world (skull as sky, blood as sea, flesh
  as earth, bones as mountains, hair as trees). This is the
  Tiamat / Puruṣa / cosmic-giant pattern at full strength in the
  Germanic substrate.
- Audhumla (the primordial cow) licking the salt-stone to free
  the first ancestor (Búri) confirms the cosmogony-from-substrate
  pattern Eliade names at
  [[eliade-sacred-and-profane#^p096]]–[[eliade-sacred-and-profane#^p100]]
  (cosmos as ordered emergence from formless ground).
- The Scandinavian Iceland-clearing case Eliade himself cites at
  [[eliade-sacred-and-profane#^p038]] — the colonisation
  recapitulating the founding cosmogony — is the
  substrate-confirmation Eliade saw and named.

### Breaks

- **Ragnarǫk breaks Eliade's annual-cosmogony pattern.** Eliade
  reads at [[eliade-sacred-and-profane#^p149]]: "At each New
  Year the cosmogony is reiterated, the world recreated."
  Norse cosmology has Ragnarǫk as final cosmogonic destruction
  *without secured cyclic rebirth*. The post-Ragnarǫk Gimli with
  Líf and Lífþrasir as sole human survivors is *new world after
  irreversible end*, not *old world recovered through cyclic
  reactualisation*. The substrate reads cosmogony as
  founding-then-ending-then-new-founding, not as
  founding-perpetually-renewed.
- This is the same break-shape that fires at [[eternal-return]];
  here it surfaces as a break in the cosmogony-pattern.
- Flag for wave 4 as `eliade-vs-norse-ragnarok-cosmogony`.

### Augmentations

- The substrate augments via **the gard-founding rite as
  domestic-scale cosmogony**. The act of taking new land
  (landnám), naming the gard, raising the gardstre, lighting
  the first ildsted, building the langhus carries the
  cosmogony-pattern at habitation scale. Eliade's frame predicts
  this at [[eliade-sacred-and-profane#^p036]]–[[eliade-sacred-and-profane#^p038]];
  the substrate fills it with substrate-specific operative
  practice (the ildsted as primary anchor; the gardstre as
  axis; the åseterett as the legal-cosmological seal).
- The substrate augments via **eschatologically-anchored
  cosmogony**. Where Eliade's pattern is annual-cyclic, the
  substrate carries forward-pointing eschatological cosmogony —
  the founding act *anticipates* the final dissolution and the
  new founding past it. This colours the substrate's brand-mythos
  application: the founding instant is not just an origin to
  return to; it is a stance toward an end the founding will
  meet.
